# Worked Example: Competitive Narrative Stress Test

**Synthetic Co**: Tessera — B2B SaaS analytics platform for retail operations. $14M ARR, growth stage.

## Context

- Founded 2020. PMF achieved Q2 2023. $14M ARR by Q1 2026.
- 38 FTEs.
- Series B raise scheduled in 6-8 months. CEO wants the competitive narrative rebuilt before the raise narrative is finalized; CFO is pushing back on a wholesale rewrite.

## Symptom (Lost Deals + Category Reframe)

- Lost 4 of last 7 enterprise deals to NewEntrant in Q1 2026 (vs 1 of 7 in Q3 2025).
- Competitive win-rate dropped from 52% to 31% over two quarters.
- NPS from active customers remains 62; usage is growing — the loss is at the top of funnel, not in delivery.
- Gartner reframed the category in March 2026 from "Retail Analytics" to "Agentic Retail Intelligence" — adopting NewEntrant's language.
- 3 board members independently asked the CEO the same question in the last 30 days: "Why aren't we agentic?"
- CEO's instinct: rewrite the narrative. CFO's pushback: "Don't chase the language — fight on outcomes."

## Trigger Event

Series B raise kickoff in 6-8 months. Tessera must win on narrative before the deck is locked, or risk being framed by analysts and prospects as the legacy alternative to NewEntrant.

## Current Tessera Narrative (verbatim, 5 Attack Surface Layers)

1. **Origin Claim**: "Built by retail operators."
2. **Differentiation Claim**: "Operator-grade depth, not analyst-grade breadth."
3. **Proof Claim**: "Used by 14 of the top 50 specialty retailers."
4. **Outcome Claim**: "$12M in margin recovered for customers in 2025."
5. **Future Claim**: "The retail operating system."

## Top 3 Competitors and Their Verbatim Narratives

### NewEntrant (the active threat — launched 2025, raised $30M Series A)

- "AI-native retail intelligence — your analyst, not your dashboard."
- "Dashboards are waiting to be agents."
- "We don't show you the chart. We make the decision and explain it."
- "Built for the next decade of retail, not the last one."

### LegacyVendor (incumbent — repositioning)

- "The enterprise-grade choice for retail analytics."
- "Trusted by 200+ retailers since 2008."
- "Secure, compliant, audit-ready out of the box."
- Wins primarily on procurement and CIO relationships, not on narrative.

### InHouse (large retailers building internal teams on Snowflake + dbt)

- "We own our data. We don't pay a vendor to interpret it for us."
- Not a vendor — but shows up in 4 of last 12 lost deals as the chosen alternative.
