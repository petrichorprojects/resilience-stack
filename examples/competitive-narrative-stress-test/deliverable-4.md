# Deliverable 4 — Competitive Narrative Cards: Tessera

**Date**: 2026-05-12
**Facilitator**: Petrichor Projects
**Format**: One sales-ready card per competitor. Print, laminate, distribute to every AE.

---

## Card 1 — NewEntrant

**Their narrative**: *"Your analyst, not your dashboard. The agentic retail intelligence platform."*

**Their attack on Tessera** (verbatim from their deck, confirmed in 4 of 7 Q1 enterprise losses):
*"Tessera was built for the dashboard era. They give you a deeper instrument. We replace the analyst headcount you can't hire. The era that needs Tessera is over."*

**Tessera's counter**:
*"Agents are gated on data-layer maturity. 60% of NewEntrant's own pilots run on Tessera-style data layers because the alternative doesn't work yet. We're not competing with the agent — we're the layer the agent needs to be useful. The buyers piloting NewEntrant are the same buyers running Tessera underneath."*

**Proof anchor**:
- 47 retail-specific data sources, 4.2x faster integration than agent-native vendors.
- NewEntrant pilot data-layer footprint: ~60% Tessera-style (verifiable from two shared customer references).

**Pivot move**: When the conversation drifts to "agentic," do not defend dashboards. Pivot to: *"Which agent are you planning to deploy, and on what data layer? Let's start there."* Reframes the meeting from instrument-vs-instrument to deployment-readiness.

---

## Card 2 — LegacyVendor

**Their narrative**: *"Enterprise-grade retail intelligence, trusted by the Fortune 500 for 20 years."*

**Their attack on Tessera** (hypothesized from their reference architecture decks):
*"Tessera is a venture-backed point solution. We're the system of record. The other 36 of the top 50 you're targeting? They've already standardized on us. Tessera fights for one of 14 seats. We hold 36."*

**Tessera's counter**:
*"LegacyVendor sells trust. Tessera sells outcome. $12M customer-validated margin recovery in 2025, methodology published, four case studies attached. Trust without outcome is the renewal you're not getting. Ask LegacyVendor for their per-customer margin-impact methodology. We'll wait."*

**Proof anchor**:
- $12M customer-validated margin recovery, published methodology.
- 100% renewal across 14 enterprise customers in trailing 24 months — same renewal stat LegacyVendor cannot match in their public case studies.
- Tessera's average 2.3 use cases live per customer vs. LegacyVendor's typical 1.1 (industry benchmark).

**Pivot move**: When LegacyVendor brings up scale or certifications, pivot to: *"What's the margin impact per customer in the deployments you're naming?"* Trust is the question they want; outcome is the question they cannot answer.

---

## Card 3 — InHouse (Snowflake / dbt build)

**Their narrative**: *"Built by your team, on your stack, owned by you. No vendor lock-in. Composable retail intelligence: your stack, your roadmap."*

**Their attack on Tessera** (paraphrased from the three Q1 lost-deal interviews citing InHouse):
*"We already have Snowflake. We have a data team. The retail-specific layer is just dbt models. We don't need a vendor — we need three quarters and we own it."*

**Tessera's counter**:
*"You don't need three quarters. You need 47 retail-specific data sources, schema reconciliation across point-of-sale, planogram, returns, and IoT, and an agent compatibility layer. Tessera shipped that in four years. The build-or-buy math isn't dbt models vs. Tessera — it's three retail-data engineers for two years vs. Tessera in 90 days. If the math still says build, we lose the deal. Most of the time the math says buy."*

**Proof anchor**:
- 47 retail-specific data source integrations — verifiable against Tessera integration catalog.
- 90-day average enterprise time-to-production for Tessera deployments.
- Three customer case studies of teams that started building in-house, switched to Tessera, and shipped the use case 4-6 quarters faster.

**Pivot move**: When InHouse comes up, do not argue against in-house competence. Pivot to: *"What's your retail data team's roadmap for 2026? Where does this initiative sit on it?"* Most data teams are six quarters backlogged. Tessera competes against their backlog, not their capability.

---

## How to Use These Cards

- Carry all three. Most enterprise deals surface at least two competitors before close.
- Lead with the **proof anchor** when pressed. Counter language without evidence is the weakness this engagement just fixed — do not reintroduce it.
- Use the **pivot move** when the conversation has drifted into the competitor's narrative frame. The pivot redirects to a question they cannot answer cleanly.
- If a deal cites a competitor not on these cards, escalate to Head of Sales — that's a Tier-1 intel signal for the next quarterly narrative refresh.
