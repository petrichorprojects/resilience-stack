## Layer 2 — Differentiation (Rebuilt)

**Old claim**: "Operator-grade depth, not analyst-grade breadth."
**New claim**: *"Tessera is the retail-data integration layer agents build on — 47 retail-specific data sources, 4.2x faster integration than agent-native vendors, and customer-validated agent compatibility."*

**Why this works**: The reframe re-positions Tessera as foundational infrastructure rather than a competing instrument. It accepts the agent era as the context and claims the layer beneath it. NewEntrant's strongest attack ("your analyst, not your dashboard") no longer lands because Tessera is no longer competing for the analyst role.

**Evidence anchor**:
- 47 retail-specific data source integrations (CPG SKU hierarchies, store-cluster schemas, in-store IoT feeds, planogram metadata, returns categorization, etc.) — verifiable against integration catalog.
- 4.2x faster integration benchmark across the three head-to-head pilots Tessera and NewEntrant both ran in 2025.
- Won-deal interview language: "the part of our stack the agents plug into."

**60-second delivery script**: *"Agents need retail data that's clean, deep, and ready. That layer doesn't exist by default — it has to be built. We've spent four years building it: 47 retail-specific sources, 4.2x faster to integrate than agent-native vendors, and verified to plug into the same agents you're piloting elsewhere. The agent era doesn't replace this layer. It depends on it. That's the work we've already done."*

## Layer 3 — Proof (Reframed)

**Old claim**: "14 of top 50 specialty retailers."
**New claim**: *"14 of the top 50 specialty retailers run Tessera in production, average 2.3 use cases live per customer, $4.1M average annual margin impact, 100% renewal in the last 24 months."*

**Why this works**: The new claim re-anchors on depth-of-deployment, which inoculates against the "stale logos" attack. The renewal stat is a freshness proxy until new enterprise logos close in H2 2026.

**Evidence anchor**:
- 2.3 use cases live per customer (verified against deployment tracker).
- $4.1M average annual margin impact per customer (derived from the published methodology — see L4).
- 100% renewal across all 14 enterprise customers in the trailing 24 months.

**60-second delivery script**: *"14 of the top 50 specialty retailers run us in production — and they don't run a single use case, they run an average of 2.3, with $4.1M average annual margin impact. Renewal across that group is 100% over the last 24 months. The logos aren't the proof. The depth is the proof."*

## Layer 4 — Outcome (Rebuilt)

**Old claim**: "$12M margin recovered in 2025."
**New claim**: *"Customer-validated $12M margin recovery in 2025 — methodology published, four customer case studies attached."*

**Why this works**: The number stays. The fatal flaw — no published methodology — is closed with a specific 30-day deliverable. The phrase "customer-validated" pre-empts the procurement objection.

**Evidence anchor**:
- Methodology document, to be published within 30 days, covering: per-customer baseline construction, attribution method, exclusion rules, third-party-auditable margin impact calculation.
- Four anonymized customer case studies attached: specialty apparel, home goods, beauty, footwear. Each with named industry segment, customer-validated margin number, and methodology footnote.

**60-second delivery script**: *"We recovered $12M in customer-validated margin across our enterprise base in 2025. The number is real because the customers signed off on it — we publish the methodology, the per-customer baseline construction, and four named-segment case studies. If procurement asks how we measured it, we hand them the document. If a competitor's outcome claim doesn't come with the methodology, you should ask why."*
