# Trajectory — Competitive Narrative Stress Test Facilitation Log: Tessera

**Session date**: 2026-05-12
**Duration**: 2h 28m
**In the room**: CEO (Naomi), Head of Marketing (Ari), Head of Sales (Diego), Head of Product (Priya)
**Facilitator**: Petrichor Projects

---

## 0:00 — Phase 1 — Pre-Work Confirmation

Evidence pack on the table: 12 lost-deal interviews, 3 won-deal interviews, NewEntrant's verbatim pitch deck (pulled via Diego), the Gartner "Agentic Retail Intelligence" reframe (March 2026), three Tessera positioning docs. Win rate: 52% in Q3 2025, 31% in Q1 2026. Four of seven enterprise losses in Q1 went to NewEntrant.

**Diego**: "We're losing the story before we're losing the demo." That set the session.

## 0:11 — Segment 1 — Narrative Landscape

Mapped four narratives side by side: Tessera ("Operator-grade depth"), NewEntrant ("Your analyst, not your dashboard"), LegacyVendor ("Enterprise-grade, Fortune 500"), InHouse ("On your Snowflake, owned by you"). Naomi flagged it: "We're describing a feature. They're describing a world."

## 0:26 — Segment 2 — Narrative Archaeology

Traced each claim to evidence. L1 Origin defensible. L2 Differentiation anchored on 47 retail-specific sources but never buyer-tested. L3 Proof: same 14 logos as 12 months ago. L4 Outcome: five-minute pause while Marketing searched for the $12M methodology document. It did not exist. L5 Future: three internal decks defined "retail operating system" three different ways.

**Facilitator**: "You have one defensible claim, two cracked, and two that collapse the moment a buyer asks 'how do you know?'"

## 0:48 — Segment 3 — Attack Surface Mapping

Scored 1-5: L1 = 5, L2 = 2, L3 = 3, L4 = 2, L5 = 1. **Composite: 13/25 (52%) — Fragile.**

Diego: "I knew L5 was bad. I didn't know L4 was bad."
Facilitator: "L4 is costing you procurement. L5 is costing you the board."

## 1:04 — Segment 4 — The Blank Check Test

**Facilitator**: "NewEntrant has unlimited budget. They put a billboard in front of every retail CIO. What does it say about you?"
**Naomi**: "Tessera was the dashboard era. We are the agent era."

Attacked L5 directly, L2 by implication. Left L1 untouched. The blank check exposed the exact two layers the scoring flagged.

## 1:19 — Segment 5 — Competitor's Best Day Simulation

Facilitator role-played NewEntrant CEO for 60 seconds against Naomi, on tape:

*"Retail leaders don't need another dashboard. They need an analyst who works at machine speed. We replace the analyst headcount you can't hire. Tessera built a beautiful instrument for the analyst era. The era ended in March. Your customers are going to ask you one question in 2026: are you agentic? You're not. We are."*

Naomi's defense, unedited: hesitation, fell back to "we have deeper retail-specific data," lost 8 seconds reaching for the $12M number. 71 seconds, ran over. Recording saved for sales enablement.

**Facilitator**: "That is what happens in every enterprise meeting. Fix the script, not the performance."

## 1:38 — Segment 6 — Narrative Reconstruction

Rebuilt L2, L4, L5 evidence-first.

**L2**: Proposed "the retail-data integration layer agents build on." Facilitator pushback: "That's a rephrase, not a reconstruction. What's the NEW evidence?" Priya pulled the integration benchmark: 47 retail-specific sources, 4.2x faster than NewEntrant on three head-to-head pilots. Re-scored 4.

**L4**: Reframed to "Customer-validated $12M margin recovery — methodology published, 4 customer case studies attached." Methodology shipping in 30 days. Conditional re-score: 3, locking to 4 on shipment.

**L5**: Killed "retail operating system." Replaced with "The retail data layer agents need to be useful." Anchor: NewEntrant's pilots use Tessera-style data layers in 60% of deployments. Partner program shipping in 60 days. Conditional re-score: 4.

## 1:54 — Segment 6b — Second Pass on L2

Diego pulled a phrase from a won-deal interview: "the part of our stack the agents plug into." Reframed L2 to add customer-confirmed agent compatibility as a second anchor. Held at 4 but with two anchors instead of one.

**Final composite: 19/25 (76%) — Holding.** +46% improvement.

## 2:08 — Segment 7 — 60-Second Delivery Test

Naomi re-delivered on timer, no notes:

*"Tessera was built by retail operators. We are the retail data layer agents need to be useful — 47 retail-specific sources, 4.2x faster integration than agent-native vendors, customer-validated $12M margin recovery with methodology published. The agent era doesn't replace the data layer. It depends on it. That's us."*

Clock: 47 seconds. No hesitation. Diego: "I would buy that." Recording saved.

## 2:18 — Segment 8 — Competitive Narrative Cards

Built three sales-ready cards: NewEntrant, LegacyVendor, InHouse. Each card: their narrative, their attack on Tessera, Tessera's counter, proof anchor, pivot move.

## 2:24 — Segment 9 — CEO Briefing + Commitment Close

One-page brief drafted. Three 90-day actions, owners locked, deadlines locked. Naomi committed publicly. Diego committed to running the Segment 5 recording in every Tuesday sales sync until the team can defend in under 60 seconds.

**Naomi, closing**: "Series B story isn't 'why we're losing.' It's 'what we did about it in 90 days.'"
**Facilitator**: "Run `competitive-blind-spot-mapping` next. The rebuild assumes you've seen NewEntrant. You haven't seen what you haven't seen."

Session closed 2:28. Deliverables shipped within 4 hours.
