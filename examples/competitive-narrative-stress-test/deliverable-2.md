# Deliverable 2 — Attack Surface Assessment (5-Layer): Tessera

**Date**: 2026-05-12
**Facilitator**: Petrichor Projects
**Scoring scale**: 1 (claim collapses under first attack) → 5 (claim holds under strongest competitor pressure)

## Layer 1 — Origin Claim

**Current claim**: "Built by retail operators." Four founders ran category planning at three top-50 specialty retailers.

**Strongest competitor attack** (NewEntrant, paraphrased from their pitch deck): *"Retail operators built dashboards. AI researchers built agents. The era that needs you is over."*

**Evidence supporting Tessera's claim**: Verified work history of the four founders. Two named customers in the 2025 case study set explicitly cite founder operator-credibility as the reason they chose Tessera over LegacyVendor.

**Crack severity**: Low. The attack reframes the era, not the credential. Operator credibility is structurally untouchable — neither NewEntrant nor LegacyVendor can claim it without lying.

**Score: 5 / 5**

**Recommended remediation**: None. Maintain and amplify. Add one new operator-customer reference per quarter to keep the claim current.

---

## Layer 2 — Differentiation Claim

**Current claim**: "Operator-grade depth, not analyst-grade breadth."

**Strongest competitor attack** (NewEntrant, verbatim from deck): *"Your analyst, not your dashboard. Tessera shows you what happened. We do something about it."*

**Evidence supporting Tessera's claim**: 47 retail-specific data sources integrated; 4.2x faster integration on the three head-to-head pilots Tessera has data for. The data exists but had not been packaged as proof.

**Crack severity**: High. "Operator-grade depth" is internal-speak. In the four enterprise losses to NewEntrant in Q1, three lost-deal interviews cite the phrase "Tessera is deeper but I need an analyst, not deeper data." The differentiation claim is technically true and commercially irrelevant as currently stated.

**Score: 2 / 5**

**Recommended remediation**: Reframe to "the retail-data integration layer agents build on." Anchor on 47 retail-specific sources and 4.2x integration benchmark. Add the customer phrase from won-deal interviews: "the part of our stack the agents plug into." See Deliverable 3 for the rebuild.

---

## Layer 3 — Proof Claim

**Current claim**: "14 of top 50 specialty retailers."

**Strongest competitor attack** (NewEntrant): *"Same 14 logos as 12 months ago. The market has moved. The logos have not."*

**Evidence supporting Tessera's claim**: 14 retailers are real, contractual, referenceable. Renewal rate on the 14 is 100% in the last 24 months.

**Crack severity**: Medium. The logos are real but the freshness signal is bad. No new enterprise logo in 12 months reads as stagnation, not stability — especially while NewEntrant is announcing three new top-50 wins.

**Score: 3 / 5**

**Recommended remediation**: Reframe the proof claim to lead with depth-of-deployment ("14 of top 50, with average 2.3 use cases live per customer, $4.1M average annual margin impact") rather than logo count. Pair with the renewal stat. Close at least two new enterprise logos in H2 2026 to restore freshness.

---

## Layer 4 — Outcome Claim

**Current claim**: "$12M margin recovered in 2025."

**Strongest competitor attack** (procurement-team perspective, hypothesized): *"$12M across how many customers, measured how, attributable how? Show me the methodology or it's marketing."*

**Evidence supporting Tessera's claim**: Internal customer-success tracking aggregates margin recovery across 11 of the 14 enterprise customers. Methodology exists in three different internal spreadsheets, never reconciled, never published.

**Crack severity**: High. In two of the four Q1 enterprise losses, procurement explicitly asked for the methodology document and Tessera could not produce it. The number is defensible. The absence of methodology is not.

**Score: 2 / 5**

**Recommended remediation**: Publish methodology document within 30 days. Attach four anonymized customer case studies with named industry segments, customer-validated margin numbers, and a third-party-auditable methodology section. Reframe claim as "Customer-validated $12M margin recovery — methodology published, 4 customer case studies attached." See Deliverable 3.

---

## Layer 5 — Future Claim

**Current claim**: "The retail operating system."

**Strongest competitor attack** (NewEntrant, post-Gartner): *"The retail operating system was the 2024 pitch. The 2026 category is Agentic Retail Intelligence. Tessera missed the rename."*

**Evidence supporting Tessera's claim**: None aligned. Three internal decks define "retail operating system" three different ways — workflow platform, intelligence platform, analytics ecosystem. The claim collapsed under Gartner's March 2026 category reframe.

**Crack severity**: Critical. This layer is currently doing damage, not zero damage. In two board meetings, three independent board members asked "why aren't we agentic?" The current future claim invites the question Tessera cannot currently answer.

**Score: 1 / 5**

**Recommended remediation**: Kill "retail operating system." Replace with "The retail data layer agents need to be useful." Anchor on: (1) NewEntrant's own pilots use Tessera-style data layers in 60% of deployments, verifiable from shared customer references; (2) integration partner program announcement within 60 days. See Deliverable 3.
