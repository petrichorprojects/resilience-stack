# Post-Engagement Notes — Tessera Competitive Narrative Stress Test

**Facilitator**: Petrichor Projects
**Session date**: 2026-05-12

---

## What Worked

Recording Segment 5 (Competitor's Best Day) was the highest-leverage move of the session. Naomi's unedited 71-second defense — hesitations, the 8-second reach for the $12M number, the fallback to "deeper retail-specific data" — is now the single most valuable sales-enablement artifact Tessera owns. Numbers on a slide do not change behavior. The CEO failing on tape does. Make recording mandatory in every future engagement.

The evidence-required reconstruction rule held the room honest. Twice during Segment 6 the team tried to ship a rephrase as a rebuild. The pushback ("That's a rephrase. What's the NEW evidence?") forced Priya to pull the integration benchmark off the shelf. That benchmark had existed for 18 months and had never been packaged as competitive proof.

## What Surprised

The outcome claim (L4) had no published methodology. Five-minute pause mid-session while Ari searched the drive for a document the team assumed existed. It did not. The $12M margin recovery number is real and unsupported in any artifact a procurement team can read. Two of the four Q1 enterprise losses had explicitly asked for that document. Tessera lost those deals partly on the absence of a one-page artifact. Next iteration: add "published outcome methodology" to the Phase 1 evidence checklist and verify the artifact actually exists, not just that the team believes it does.

The won-deal interview language ("the part of our stack the agents plug into") was the unlock for the L2 rebuild and Tessera had never used the phrase in marketing. Customers gave the team the differentiation language 12 months ago and the team had not heard it. Move won-deal language excavation up to Phase 1 in future runs.

## What To Do Differently

Insist on the **Chief Customer Officer in the room**. Tessera's CCO was traveling; Diego covered champion-handoff. The CCO would have caught the methodology gap in Phase 1 and shortened the L4 rebuild by 20 minutes. For any Series-B-stage engagement: CCO attendance is mandatory.

**Pre-pull won-deal language verbatim before session start.** We pre-pulled 12 lost-deal interviews and only 3 won-deal interviews. Asymmetric — won-deal interviews are where the rebuild evidence lives. Equal weighting on pre-work going forward: at least 5 won-deal interviews with verbatim language tagged for differentiation, proof, and outcome.

**Lock the durability target before Segment 6, not during.** Naomi pushed for a higher score mid-rebuild, which is the right instinct but introduces moving goalposts. State the target at the start of Segment 6 — minimum 30%, target 50% — so the rebuild loop terminates cleanly. We ran one extra pass that should have been the first pass.

Action 1 (publish methodology in 30 days) was committed with no procurement-team input. Validate with two friendly procurement contacts before publication — otherwise we ship a marketing artifact instead of a procurement-defeating one.
