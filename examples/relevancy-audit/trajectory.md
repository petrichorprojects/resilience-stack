# Trajectory — Relevancy Audit Facilitation Log: Synthetic Co

**Session date**: 2026-05-12
**Duration**: 2h 31m
**In the room**: Founder/CEO (Maya), VP Marketing (Devan), Head of Sales (Rasheed), Head of Product (Jen)
**Facilitator**: Petrichor Projects

---

## 0:00 — Phase 1 — Pre-Work Confirmation

Confirmed data inventory before opening the diagnostic. Synthetic Co arrived with: 4 quarters of win/loss with reasons coded, 18 customer surveys with verbatim NPS, 3 competitor positioning statements pulled from public sites, the Q4 2025 analyst quadrant report.

**Facilitator**: "What's missing from this stack that should be here?"
**Maya**: "Lapsed prospects. We have current customer voices but not the ones who walked."
Logged as a gap. Proceeded — sufficient evidence for Stages 1-2 detection; flagged as a Phase 2 caveat.

Confirmed the verbatim positioning statement: *"The leading legal operations platform for modern firms."* Five seconds of silence in the room after Devan read it aloud. That silence became the audit.

## 0:14 — Phase 2 Segment 1 — Customer Amnesia Test

**Facilitator**: "If I asked your top 5 customers what Synthetic Co does, in their words — what would they actually say?"
**Rasheed**: "They'd say we run their operations stack — billing, compliance, intake."
**Facilitator**: "That's what you do. What would they SAY? Read the verbatim NPS — bring me three exact phrases."
Devan pulled the surveys. Phrases that came back: "the spreadsheet replacement," "what our paralegals open every morning," "the compliance reports we use for audits." None of them sounded like "leading platform." None of them sounded "modern."

**Finding called in real time**: Description Gap = 7/10. Customers describe a tool. Positioning describes a category.

## 0:31 — Phase 2 Segment 2 — Buyer Language Audit

Ran the top-12 buyer-survey terms against the verbatim positioning. Result: **zero of the top 12 terms appeared in the positioning statement.** Not "compliance," not "intake," not "audit-ready," not "paralegal workflow." The positioning was abstracted away from every word the buyer actually used.

**Facilitator**: "Your positioning was written for analysts. Your buyers are paralegal supervisors and ops managers. The language gap is the audit."

## 0:48 — Phase 3 Segment 3 — Stage Signal Reading

Walked the room through the Stage 1/2/3 thresholds against their numbers:
- Win rate 38% → 24% (-37% relative) — Stage 2 confirmed
- Cycle length 47 → 62 days (+32%) — Stage 2 confirmed
- Share of voice flat 18 months despite 2× spend — Stage 1→2 transition
- Analyst language "Leader" → "Established Player" — Stage 2 confirmed
- 3 lost deals mention an unknown competitor in 1 quarter — Stage 2 confirmed
- Buyer-language match <40% — Stage 3 indicator

**Jen**: "We're not Stage 1."
**Facilitator**: "Correct. You're Stage 2, borderline Stage 3. Sales is telling you with the loss data; the analyst is telling you with the report; your own surveys are telling you with the language. The instruments all agree."

## 1:02 — Phase 3 Segment 4 — Unknown Competitor

**Facilitator**: "Name NewEntrant out loud. What do you know about them?"
Silence. Rasheed: "Funded 2024. AI-native legal ops. We don't have a sales playbook against them."
**Facilitator**: "Three losses in one quarter mentioned them by name. Your win/loss CRM field doesn't have a row for them. That's not bad luck. That's a structural intel gap."

## 1:14 — Phase 3 Segment 5 — Competitive Drift

Walked the 3-axis competitive map: LegacyVendor (trusted/safe), NewEntrant (AI-native), InHouse (build-your-own). Synthetic Co lost a deal to all three in the same quarter. The pattern: Synthetic Co is no longer the obvious choice on any axis. LegacyVendor wins safety. NewEntrant wins narrative. InHouse wins control.

**Maya**: "We're squeezed."
**Facilitator**: "You're undifferentiated. That's worse than squeezed. Squeezed is a position. Undifferentiated is the absence of one."

## 1:27 — Phase 4 Segment 6 — "AI-Native" Claim Test

**Facilitator**: "NewEntrant says AI-native. Three lost deals cite it. Is AI workflow a real buyer priority or a marketing claim that hasn't been validated against actual buying behavior?"
**Jen**: "We don't know."
**Facilitator**: "That's the most useful answer of the session. You don't know — and the cost of not knowing is the next four quarters of win rate."
Logged as a Phase 7 action: validate or kill, requires `customer-truth-extraction` skill.

## 1:41 — Phase 4 Segment 7 — Recent Wins Are Wrong-Reason Wins

Audited the 3 recent wins. Reasons: integrations, compliance reports, partner stickiness. None of those reasons map to the verbatim positioning. The product is winning on capabilities the positioning doesn't claim.

**Facilitator**: "Your marketing is selling one product. Your sales team is closing a different one. The disconnect isn't between you and the market — it's between your positioning and your own go-to-market."

## 1:54 — Phase 5 Segment 8 — Loss Pattern

Three losses, three different reasons: AI workflow (NewEntrant), safety (LegacyVendor), control (InHouse). The losses spread across all three competitive vectors. There is no single thing to fix. There is a positioning vacuum.

## 2:06 — Phase 6 Segment 9 — Decay Score Synthesis

Calculated the composite: **4.2 / 10 (FRAGILE)**. Stage 2 Disconnect, borderline Stage 3. Decay Clock estimate: 9-12 months of relevancy capital before crossing into Stage 3 Displacement at current trajectory.

**Facilitator**: "Nine months means the September board meeting. Either you have a refreshed positioning, a NewEntrant playbook, and validated AI-claim data — or your win rate has another 8-10 points to give."

## 2:19 — Phase 7 — Deliverable Generation

Generated the 5-document deliverable package in-session: Positioning Decay Map (gap, drift, signals), Signal Refresh Roadmap (3 actions, 90 days), Stage Classification (evidence per indicator), Competitive Position Snapshot (4-way map), Scorecard (executive summary).

**Maya, closing**: "We've been treating this as a marketing problem. It's a structural detection problem."
**Facilitator**: "Correct. The fix is structural. The roadmap is in the deliverables. The next skill to run is `competitive-narrative-stress-test` — before you rewrite the positioning, stress-test the story against NewEntrant's."

Session closed at 2:31. Deliverables shipped same day.
