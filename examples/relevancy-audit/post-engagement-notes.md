# Post-Engagement Notes — Synthetic Co Relevancy Audit

**Facilitator**: Petrichor Projects
**Session date**: 2026-05-12

---

## What Worked

The verbatim positioning read-aloud in the first 14 minutes did 70% of the diagnostic work. The room went silent after Devan said "The leading legal operations platform for modern firms" out loud. That silence was the audit. Every subsequent phase was confirmation, which let the rest of the session run at pace because the team was no longer defending the status quo.

The buyer-language audit (0 / 12 terms) landed harder than the win-rate decline. Numbers about win rate read as "tough market." Zero out of twelve is not interpretable — it is a measurement. The team could not argue with it because they had brought the surveys themselves. Pre-work paid off; facilitator-supplied data would have been disputed.

## What Surprised

The recent-wins audit was the underestimated phase. The team came in thinking the wins were positive evidence. Reading the win reasons aloud (integrations, compliance reports, partner stickiness) against the positioning statement produced the second silence — the recognition that the product was winning on capabilities the positioning didn't claim. That gap is more diagnostic than the loss data because it reveals the team is selling a different product than its marketing describes. Move this earlier in future sessions — currently late Phase 4, should be early Phase 3.

The Head of Product asked the most useful question of the day, unprompted: "We don't know if AI workflow is a real buyer priority." That admission opened the path to Action 3. Founders are usually the leaders least willing to admit "we don't know" — Jen volunteering it shifted the team posture for the back half. Next time, explicitly invite the "what don't we know" question earlier.

## What To Do Differently

Pre-work should include lapsed prospects. We flagged the gap in Phase 1 and proceeded anyway. Action 3 (the AI-native verdict) will be weaker because we lack the sharpest signal source — the buyers who actively chose NewEntrant. Make lapsed-prospect inclusion a Phase 1 hard prerequisite for any Stage 2-suspected engagement.

Head of Customer Success was not in the room. Maya elected to keep the session compact at four leaders. CS would have surfaced churn-risk signals and would have shortened Phase 4 by 15 minutes. For future Stage 2 audits, CS attendance is mandatory.

We ran 31 minutes long. Phase 5 (competitive map) deserved more time; Phase 7 (deliverable generation) deserved less — the deliverables ship post-session anyway, and walking them line-by-line was a bandwidth tax on a team that had already absorbed the verdict. Next iteration: spend Phase 7 only on the scorecard, ship the full deliverables async within 4 hours of session close.

Action 3's decision rule ("5+ of 12 buyers cite AI workflow unprompted") was set at 1:34 with team energy fading. Right call, wrong timing. Set decision rules at Phase 6, not Phase 7.
