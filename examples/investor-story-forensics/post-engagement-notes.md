# Post-Engagement Notes — Investor Story Forensics: Aperture

**Date**: 2026-05-12
**Facilitator**: Petrichor Projects

Facilitator-only notes. What worked, what surprised, what to do differently when running this skill again. Not for the client deck.

---

## What Worked

**Split-Room Story Coherence Test (Segment 6) surfaced the single most consequential disagreement in the leadership team in 12 minutes.** Mateo and Wren in one room, Lin + Hollis + Suri in the other, told the trajectory story independently. The CEO + Product side said +95% Y/Y was realistic; the Finance + Sales + CS side said it was a stretch and trailing is +68%. The team had been operating with a tacit two-narrative split for at least two quarters. The exercise made the split undeniable in a way no spreadsheet review had. By Segment 7 the room had aligned on +68% as the headline. The skill cannot ship a deck without this test — the divergence will not surface in 1:1 prep work.

**Pre-pulling the bear case in writing before the session started.** Reading the bear-case memo aloud at 0:09 created an immediate "wait, that's actually accurate" reaction across the executive team. The bear case as facilitator-written cold beat anything the team would have generated themselves, because the team is structurally invested in not seeing it. Recommend keeping this as a mandatory pre-work artifact.

**Reference Arc Audit (Segment 7) on a per-customer relationship-arc basis, not just a logo list.** The deck named five reference customers. The logo list looked fine. The relationship arcs — last meaningful champion touchpoint, current contract motion, account health — surfaced four diligence-risk accounts the executive team had not connected to the Series C narrative. Suri's input was load-bearing here. Future runs should bring CS in earlier, not at 0:42.

## What Surprised

**Two of five named reference customers had champion changes nobody internally tracked against the Series C reference plan.** Account A's new VP has been in seat 142 days. Account B was acquired 91 days ago. CS knew about both. Sales knew about Account A. The executive team had not connected the dots to the reference list in the deck. This is a process gap — the Series C reference plan should have been a CS-owned artifact updated quarterly, not an artifact assembled from the deck logo wall at fundraise time.

**The price-basis confusion ($400M post = 10x of what?) was unresolved inside the CFO's head, not just on the slide.** Lin defaulted to "forward" when asked but had not run the trailing math. The deck inherited the ambiguity from the leadership team's own undecided multiple basis. This is the kind of thing that breaks in a partner meeting because nobody on the team has practiced answering it under pressure.

**The non-automotive 38% pilot churn was a known data point in product and CS but had never been packaged as a narrative input.** Wren and Suri both volunteered the number when asked. It had not been on a leadership dashboard, never escalated, never surfaced in board materials. The data was there; the synthesis to "this kills the J-curve claim" was not.

## What to Do Differently

**Pre-pull the reference customer status list as a mandatory pre-work artifact, owned by Head of CS.** This run, the reference arc data arrived during the session. Future runs should require: last 4 quarters of named-customer relationship arcs, with champion-change timestamps, contract motion (renewal / expansion / paused / churned), and a CS-owned health score. Surfaces the diligence-risk reference customers before the room opens.

**Include Head of Customer Success from the start of Segment 1, not at 0:42 by phone.** Too much of the most consequential evidence sits in CS's head. Future runs: CS in the room from minute zero, with the customer status list printed and on the table.

**Record a mock reference call in Segment 7 or 8.** Tessera's session benefited from the Segment 5 "Competitor's Best Day Simulation" recording — it became sales enablement material the team used after the session. The equivalent here is a mock reference call: facilitator role-plays the diligence partner, Head of CS role-plays the reference customer, recording saved. Trains the team for the actual partner reference call before it happens, and surfaces the gaps in the relationship arc that a coaching pass won't cover.

**Push back harder, earlier, on the "we'll fix the numbers before the deck ships" promise.** Lin agreed at 0:24 to restate the trailing numbers. By 1:31 the deck v0.3 still went to two firms with the old numbers. The remediation deadline (14 days for trailing-data slide rebuild) needs a 24-hour deck-freeze rule: no further partner sends until the restated deck is in hand. Add to standard commitment template.
