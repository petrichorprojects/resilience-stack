# Deliverable 4 — Investor FAQ Preparation: Aperture

**Date**: 2026-05-12
**Facilitator**: Petrichor Projects

25 questions a sophisticated Series C partner will ask, grouped by the five claim classes. Each question shows the underlying pattern it tests, the honest answer (what the data says), and the strategically framed answer (how to deliver it in the room without losing the partner).

The honest answer and the strategically framed answer should be the same fact, told two ways. Anything where they diverge in substance is a tell that the deck is still hiding something.

## Why-We-Win (5 questions)

**Q1. What's the operator origin actually worth in CAC or sales cycle terms?**
- *Pattern tested*: Is the operator story real, or marketing?
- *Honest*: Sales cycle for operator-referred logos is 38% shorter than cold pipeline. ~22% of new logos in 2025 sourced via operator network.
- *Framed*: "Operator origin compresses the buy cycle by roughly 5 months and drives a quarter of new logos through a channel competitors cannot replicate. We'll show the cohort split in the data room."

**Q2. What stops Cognex or Keyence from copying the operator-built positioning?**
- *Pattern tested*: Is the moat structural or rhetorical?
- *Honest*: They can copy the language. They cannot copy the founder bench or the operator-network referral motion built over 5 years.
- *Framed*: "Anyone can say 'built by operators.' The proof is who answers the support call at 11pm on a line-down event. Our founders have. That's why the operator network refers."

**Q3. How does the operator advantage scale past the first 50 customers?**
- *Pattern tested*: Founder-led moat decay risk.
- *Honest*: The operator network compounds — every new customer becomes a referral node. But scaling the founder bench requires hiring more operators, not engineers.
- *Framed*: "Our Series C hiring plan is operator-weighted: 40% of new CS and product hires come from QA / plant-floor backgrounds. We're scaling the moat, not diluting it."

**Q4. Who do you lose deals to today and why?**
- *Pattern tested*: Self-awareness about competition.
- *Honest*: Cognex on price in the long-tail; Keyence on installed-base inertia; AI-native upstart on the agent narrative.
- *Framed*: "We lose to Cognex in deals under $80K ACV where price is the only variable. We lose to Keyence when they're already the line scanner. We lose to AI-native vendors when the buyer is the CIO, not the plant manager. Our wedge is plant-manager buying; that's where we expand."

**Q5. What's the win rate trend over the last 4 quarters?**
- *Pattern tested*: Are you losing share?
- *Honest*: Plant-manager-led deals: 51% win rate, stable. CIO-led deals: 28% win rate, declining.
- *Framed*: "In our core buyer archetype — plant manager — we win half the time and the rate is stable. We've been losing CIO-led deals in 2025; that's why our 2026 sales motion is doubling down on plant-floor selling, not chasing the CIO."

## Why-Now (4 questions)

**Q6. Every AI-native inspection vendor pitches this same Why-Now. What's specific to Aperture?**
- *Pattern tested*: Is the thesis generic?
- *Honest*: The thesis is generic; the wedge is the buyer archetype (operator, not CIO) and the regulatory tailwind in automotive QA (false-positive cost asymmetry).
- *Framed*: "The category thesis is widely shared. Our specific bet is that the operator buyer wins this category — they own the false-positive cost. We sell to the person who pays for the mistake."

**Q7. What's the regulatory tailwind specifically?**
- *Pattern tested*: Depth of category understanding.
- *Honest*: Automotive functional safety standards (ISO 26262, ASPICE) tightened false-positive disclosure requirements in 2024-2025. Plant managers carry liability.
- *Framed*: "ISO 26262 and ASPICE tightened false-positive accountability in 2024. The plant manager now owns the audit. That's our buyer."

**Q8. What if the agent layer commoditizes inspection software?**
- *Pattern tested*: Existential risk awareness.
- *Honest*: Agents need the data layer. The risk is real but lower than for analytics vendors — inspection has hard physical-world constraints that an agent doesn't solve.
- *Framed*: "Agents accelerate the analyst. They don't replace the camera on the line. We own the camera-to-decision path. The agent sits on top of us, not in place of us."

**Q9. Why now vs. 3 years ago or 3 years from now?**
- *Pattern tested*: Capital-formation cycle timing.
- *Honest*: 3 years ago: regulatory framework not in place, operator buying not unlocked. 3 years from now: incumbent retrofits will close the window.
- *Framed*: "Three years ago the regulatory pressure wasn't there. Three years from now the incumbents will have AI retrofits in market. The window to win the operator buyer is 2026-2028."

## Why-Us (5 questions)

**Q10. What's your trailing four-quarter NRR?**
- *Pattern tested*: Cherry-picking detection.
- *Honest*: 117%.
- *Framed*: "Trailing four-quarter NRR is 117%. Best quarter was 130% in Q2 2025, driven by two specific expansion deals; we expect 2-3 of those per year at current ACV but don't project them into the headline."

**Q11. How many OEMs are currently active, not historical?**
- *Pattern tested*: Logo-count integrity.
- *Honest*: 11 active; 14 historical; 3 churned or paused since Q2 2025.
- *Framed*: "11 active enterprise OEMs today. 14 historical. Three rolled off in 2025-2026 — one acquisition spin-down, one budget freeze, one competitive displacement. We can walk through each."

**Q12. Why did the 3 OEMs roll off?**
- *Pattern tested*: Post-loss honesty.
- *Honest*: One acquired entity spun us down with the acquiring vendor's tool. One budget-froze in late 2025; in re-engagement now. One displaced by Cognex on price after a 38-month relationship.
- *Framed*: "One was a corporate action we couldn't control. One is a recoverable budget freeze with a 2026 re-engagement plan. One we lost on price and have post-mortem'd: we won't renew that deal architecture."

**Q13. What's your gross retention?**
- *Pattern tested*: Floor of the retention story.
- *Honest*: 96% trailing-four.
- *Framed*: "Gross retention is 96%. The 117% NRR sits on a 96% floor. Healthier shape than higher-NRR businesses with leaky gross retention."

**Q14. Show me cohort retention by entry year.**
- *Pattern tested*: Cohort durability.
- *Honest*: 2022 cohort: 142% net retention at month 36. 2023 cohort: 128% at month 24. 2024 cohort: 119% at month 12. Each cohort softer than the last.
- *Framed*: "Cohorts hold above 115% at every age band we've measured. 2022 is at 142% at month 36. Newer cohorts come in lower but compound on a higher base — net dollar contribution is increasing per cohort."

## Why-This-Trajectory (6 questions)

**Q15. What's your trailing four-quarter growth rate, not Y/Y?**
- *Pattern tested*: The +95% number.
- *Honest*: +68%.
- *Framed*: "+68% trailing four quarters. The +95% Y/Y in the deck is Q1 2025 vs. Q1 2024 — accurate, but a single-quarter compare. The number we underwrite the plan against is +68%."

**Q16. Is growth decelerating?**
- *Pattern tested*: Trajectory shape.
- *Honest*: Yes — Q4 2024 was +74%, Q1 2026 is +64%. Trailing-four held at +68% by mid-cycle quarters.
- *Framed*: "Quarterly growth has moved from the mid-70s to the mid-60s. The decel is real and consistent with the law of large numbers at our ACV. The plan assumes +60-65% through Series C year, not +95."

**Q17. How much of revenue is non-automotive today?**
- *Pattern tested*: Diversification reality.
- *Honest*: 6%.
- *Framed*: "6% today. We're a single-vertical company building three optionality bets. The optionality is not in the plan as load-bearing revenue for the Series C year — it's an upside lever."

**Q18. What's the pilot-to-paid conversion in non-automotive?**
- *Pattern tested*: Pilot funnel honesty.
- *Honest*: 62% (38% churn).
- *Framed*: "Currently 62%. Below our automotive conversion of 84%. We've identified the failure patterns — regulatory qualification in aerospace, validation cycles in pharma, integration timeline in semiconductor — and we're shipping a pilot conversion playbook in the next 90 days to address each."

**Q19. When does aerospace become material revenue?**
- *Pattern tested*: Roadmap concreteness.
- *Honest*: Targeting 5 paid customers by month 18 of Series C; ~$3-4M ARR.
- *Framed*: "Our internal milestone is 5 paid aerospace customers by month 18 — roughly $3-4M ARR. If we hit, we proceed. If we miss, we de-prioritize and concentrate the bet elsewhere. We won't carry vertical investment that doesn't earn it."

**Q20. What's your pipeline coverage for the Series C year plan?**
- *Pattern tested*: Plan credibility.
- *Honest*: 2.1x for the +69% growth needed. Healthy for trailing-four, light for the +95% headline.
- *Framed*: "2.1x coverage against the +69% forward plan. The plan is built on trailing-four math, not on the +95% number. Coverage is sufficient for the plan we're underwriting."

## Why-This-Price (5 questions)

**Q21. $400M post — what's the multiple basis?**
- *Pattern tested*: Forward vs. trailing transparency.
- *Honest*: 5.6x trailing, 10x forward (end-of-Series-C-year ARR of ~$71M).
- *Framed*: "5.6x trailing, 10x forward. Forward ARR assumes +69% growth in the year, consistent with our trailing-four. We're not asking partners to underwrite a snap-back."

**Q22. Comparable rounds at this multiple in vertical SaaS in 2025-2026?**
- *Pattern tested*: Comp set rigor.
- *Honest*: Three comparables at 9-11x forward, two at 6-8x. Our profile sits in the middle.
- *Framed*: "Five comparables in the last 12 months in vertical SaaS at our growth and NRR profile. Range is 6-11x forward. We're asking for 10x — top of the range, with the operator moat and the AI-native thesis as the premium justification."

**Q23. What gets you to the $1.5B exit number?**
- *Pattern tested*: Realism of exit math.
- *Honest*: ~$150M ARR at 10x or ~$100M at 15x — requires three more years at +60-70% growth.
- *Framed*: "Honest answer: a $1.5B exit in 36 months requires sustained +60-70% growth on top of Series C. We don't put that on the partner slide — we underwrite the next 18 months. The exit math sits in the board narrative; the partner narrative is the 18-month milestone grid."

**Q24. Would you take $350M post with a clean structure?**
- *Pattern tested*: Negotiation flexibility.
- *Honest*: Yes, with milestone step-ups to $400M.
- *Framed*: "We'd take a clean $350M with milestone step-ups — aerospace traction, trailing-four NRR re-anchor — that unlock $400M during the round. Structure that aligns capital to proof."

**Q25. What's the bear case on Aperture?**
- *Pattern tested*: Self-awareness; willingness to name the weakness first.
- *Honest*: Concentrated automotive single-vertical, decelerating growth, reference-customer transitions in flight, non-automotive pilot churn at 38%.
- *Framed*: "The bear case is single-vertical concentration, growth decel into the 60s, four of five reference customers in transition or renewal motion, and 38% non-automotive pilot churn. We name each and we have a 90-day remediation plan on the table. The bull case is the operator moat, 117% trailing NRR, 96% gross retention, and a buyer archetype no competitor is built for. We think the bull case wins. We want partners who agree after seeing the bear."
