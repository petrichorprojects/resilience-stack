## 0:00 — Phase 1 — Pre-Work Confirmation

On the table: current Series C deck (v0.3), last 4 quarters of financials, customer status list (active / at-risk / paused / churned), reference customer relationship arc for the 5 named in the deck, the 10 most common diligence questions from the Series B raise, and the bear-case memo a sophisticated short would write tomorrow.

**Facilitator**: "The 130% NRR slide — is that trailing four quarters or best quarter?"
**Lin**: "Best quarter. Q2 2025."
**Facilitator**: "What's trailing four?"
**Lin**: "117."
**Facilitator**: "Then 130 is a marketing number. It will not survive a data room. We will recompute trailing in Segment 1 and the deck will reflect it before Segment 9."

Read aloud the bear-case headline drafted pre-session: *"Aperture is a single-vertical, single-buyer-archetype business growing slower than the deck claims, with concentrated reference risk and a non-automotive expansion thesis that is failing in the data."* Mateo did not laugh.

## 0:11 — Segment 1 — Forensic Evidence Wall Review

Five claim classes posted to the wall with evidence behind each. Within 9 minutes the wall had five red dots:

- Why-We-Win — operator origin defensible, but unspecified vs. competitors.
- Why-Now — AI-native inspection thesis is true; specific to Aperture is undefended.
- Why-Us — 130% NRR is best-quarter; 14 of 25 OEM claim is 11 of 25 active; 3 OEMs have churned or paused since Q2 2025.
- Why-This-Trajectory — +95% Y/Y is the headline, trailing is +68%, and the J-curve to aerospace/pharma/semiconductor isn't in the data yet.
- Why-This-Price — $400M post at 10x trailing ARR is internally inconsistent with the +68% trailing number.

**Hollis**: "Three of those numbers are in the deck I sent to two firms last week."
**Facilitator**: "Then those firms are already modeling the bear case. We can still get ahead of it. Barely."

## 0:24 — Segment 2 — The Cross-Examination Protocol

Walked claim by claim. Each claim taken as if a diligence partner was reading it aloud.

**On Why-Us (NRR)**:
**Facilitator**: "What's the NRR number a partner will pull from your data room — not the slide?"
**Lin**: "Trailing four is 117."
**Facilitator**: "Then 117 is the number in the deck. 130 stays only if it is footnoted as 'best quarter Q2 2025' and the trailing is the headline."

**On Why-Us (logos)**:
**Facilitator**: "14 of 25 top global automotive OEMs. Define 'top.' Define 'active.'"
**Hollis**: "JD Power top 25 by 2024 production volume. Active means contract in force."
**Facilitator**: "Then 11. Three rolled off. Don't make the diligence partner find that on a reference call. Put it on the slide as '11 active, 14 historical' or the deck dies in the data room."

**On Why-This-Trajectory**:
**Facilitator**: "+95% Y/Y. Trailing or best?"
**Lin**: "+95 was Q1 2025 vs. Q1 2024. Trailing is +68."
**Facilitator**: "+68 is still a real number. +95 is a number a partner will check in 90 seconds and you'll lose the room. Use +68."

**On Why-This-Price**:
**Facilitator**: "$400M at 10x. 10x of what?"
**Lin**: "Forward ARR."
**Facilitator**: "Forward by how much?"
**Lin**: "End of Series C year."
**Facilitator**: "So you're asking for a 10x multiple on a number you haven't earned, while showing a trailing growth rate that doesn't support it. A growth investor reads that as ambition; a fundamentals investor reads that as a markdown coming."

No deflection accepted. Three claims downgraded inside 50 minutes.

## 0:42 — Segment 3 — Why-You-Win Interrogation + Trajectory Shape Alignment

Suri joined by phone. Walked the trajectory shape: deck implies J-curve into aerospace/pharma/semiconductor; data shows linear in automotive with a non-automotive pilot funnel that converts at 62%. **38% pilot churn outside automotive.**

**Wren**: "Aerospace pilots stall on regulatory qualification. Pharma stalls on validation cycles. Semiconductor we keep losing on integration timelines."
**Facilitator**: "Then the J-curve isn't a trajectory claim, it's a roadmap claim. Don't sell a J-curve you can't show. Sell a linear-plus-optionality: durable automotive core, three vertical bets with stated conversion targets, and an honest pilot funnel."
**Mateo**: "That's a downgrade."
**Facilitator**: "It's an upgrade to a story that survives diligence. A J-curve a partner can't model is a deck that doesn't get to a term sheet."

## 1:01 — Segment 4 — Investor Archetype Calibrator

Mapped four archetypes against current claims:

- **Growth investor**: wants the J-curve. Current deck pitches them. Trailing data does not support it. Verdict: mismatch.
- **Momentum investor**: wants the headline numbers — 130 NRR, +95, 14 of 25. Current deck pitches them. Numbers won't survive the data room. Verdict: dangerous mismatch.
- **Fundamentals investor**: wants trailing-four NRR, trailing growth, net dollar retention by cohort, unit economics. Current deck does not pitch them. Verdict: untapped fit.
- **Contrarian investor**: wants the non-obvious thesis. "AI-native inspection wins the operator layer of vertical manufacturing" is contrarian. Deck doesn't lead with it. Verdict: untapped fit.

**Facilitator**: "Your strongest fit is the audience you aren't pitching. Your worst fit is the audience the current deck targets hardest."
