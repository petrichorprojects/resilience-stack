## Top 3 90-Day Actions

1. **Re-anchor 3 reference customers + replace 1.** Four of five named customers carry diligence risk: two champion changes (Accounts A, B), one renewal-negotiation discount ask (Account C), one paused (Account D). Re-anchor A and B with new champions through CEO + Head of CS outreach. Replace D with an active expanding account. Run a mock reference call per account before partner exposure. Owner: **Head of Customer Success (Suri) + CEO (Mateo)**. Deadline: **60 days**. Success: 5-of-5 named references can deliver an unprompted strong call to a diligence partner; relationship-arc documentation in the data-room binder.

2. **Recompute trailing-data slides + restate the deck.** Replace 130% NRR with 117% trailing, replace +95% Y/Y with +68% trailing, replace 14 OEMs with "11 active, 14 historical," restate the price basis as "5.6x trailing / 10x forward." Add cohort retention, gross retention, and a milestones-to-valuation step-up grid. Owner: **CFO (Lin)**. Deadline: **14 days**. Success: deck v0.4 reflects trailing-data math on every headline metric; no slide contains a single-quarter or single-deal cherry-pick without footnoted context.

3. **Non-automotive pilot conversion playbook.** 38% pilot churn outside automotive is the leading indicator of the J-curve thesis failing in the data. Ship a documented playbook: pre-pilot qualification criteria, in-pilot success metrics, time-to-conversion targets by vertical (aerospace, pharma, semiconductor), kill criteria. Re-cut the pipeline against the playbook. Owner: **Head of Product (Wren) + Head of Customer Success (Suri)**. Deadline: **90 days**. Success: playbook published, all in-flight non-automotive pilots re-qualified against it, new pilot-start gating live; target pilot-to-paid conversion 75% by month 6 (from current 62%).

## Risk if No Action

The Series C deck enters partner meetings with three cherry-picked headline numbers. A sophisticated diligence partner recomputes within 90 minutes of data-room access and prices Aperture at $280M-$320M post on trailing — a **1-2 turn valuation discount** vs. the $400M ask. In the more adverse scenario, the round dies in partner meeting because the +95% claim recomputes to +68% in real time and the reference call to Account D returns "paused since February." That outcome closes the 10-12 month Series C window and forces a bridge round at worse terms. Board confidence in the team's diligence preparedness erodes ahead of the next board cycle.

**Trailing-quarter NRR.** Target: **122% by end of Q3 2026.** Current: 117% (trailing-four ending Q1 2026).
