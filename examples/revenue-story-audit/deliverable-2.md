# Deliverable 2 — Growth Mechanics Map

**Company**: NorthStack
**ARR**: $20M (Q1 2026)
**Audit date**: 2026-05-12

This document shows where NorthStack's revenue actually comes from, why it stays, and where the single points of failure sit. It replaces the "diversified, sales-led, durable" framing with the measured mechanics.

---

## 1. ARR Composition by Customer

| Cohort | Share of ARR | Customer count | Avg ACV |
|--------|--------------|----------------|---------|
| Top 1 | **22%** | 1 | $4.4M |
| Top 2-10 | 42% | 9 | $933K |
| Top 11+ | 36% | ~110 | $65K |

**Read**: Two-thirds of the revenue ($12.8M of $20M) sits in 10 customers. The Top 1 alone equals the Top 11+ tail combined plus 5 points. This is not a diversified book. It is a concentrated book with a healthy mid-market tail underneath.

The Top 11+ cohort is the actual diversification story. 110 customers, no single one above 2% of ARR, gross retention 94%. That is the part of the book Series C diligence will reward — if it is named correctly.

## 2. New ARR Channel Attribution (System-Measured, Q1 2026 TTM)

The Q4 2025 deck claimed 60% outbound. System-attributed (multi-touch, first-touch corrected, partner-source verified):

| Channel | Claimed | Measured | Delta |
|---------|---------|----------|-------|
| Outbound | 60% | **40%** | -20 pts |
| Inbound | (unspecified) | **38%** | +38 pts disclosed |
| Partner referrals | (unspecified) | **22%** | +22 pts disclosed |

**Mechanism behind the gap**: rep-coded attribution at deal close. The $1 Trace Exercise (trajectory Segment 2) confirmed the pattern with a single dollar — partner-sourced lead, inbound-converted, outbound-stamped at close. Three of six traced dollars showed the same pattern. The outbound team is closing deals it did not source. The narrative gave it source credit it had not earned.

**Verbatim correction**: The Q1 2026 sales pitch must remove the "60% outbound" claim. The reconciled framing: *"40% outbound, 38% inbound, 22% partner referrals — three channels, none dominant, each with distinct unit economics."* That framing is both honest and stronger for diligence — three working channels is a better story than one dominant channel.

## 3. Why Dollars Stay — Retention Mechanics

Last full-quarter measured NRR: **96%**. Decomposition:

| Component | Contribution to NRR |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Gross retention (logo) | 94% |
| Expansion (new BUs, seats, modules) | +9.5% |
| Contraction (downgrades, renegotiations) | -5.0% |
| Churn (dollars) | -2.5% |
| **Net** | **96%** |

**Read by cohort**:

- **Top 1**: -8% effective price in Q1 2026 (renegotiation). NRR contribution: negative.
- **Top 2-10**: 3 customers in active churn-signal status (usage decline, exec turnover, M&A consolidation). Aggregate cohort NRR: ~98%.
- **Top 11+**: Gross retention 94%, expansion +12%, NRR ~104%. **This cohort is the bright spot.** It is durable, diversified, and expanding. The narrative does not currently feature it.

**Mechanism for the 96 → target 105 path**: hold Top 11+ at current 104%, recover Top 2-10 to 100%+ by mitigating the 3 churn-signal accounts, accept Top 1 as a flat-to-negative contributor and reduce its share of the book through new-logo growth in the tail.

## 4. Why Dollars Enter — New Logo Mechanics

Q1 2026 new logos by source (38% of which came from a single partner referral channel that has gone silent since February 2026):

| Source | % of Q1 2026 new logos | Status |
|--------|------------------------|--------|
| Partner channel A (FinOps Network) | 38% | **Silent since Feb 2026** |
| Outbound (SDR-sourced) | 31% | Active |
| Inbound (content + paid) | 24% | Active |
| Other partner / referral | 7% | Mixed |

**Single point of failure**: Partner channel A was 38% of Q1 new logos. It went silent. No replacement plan in motion as of audit date. The +120% forecast assumed this channel held. The reconciled +60-75% forecast assumes it does not return in 2026.

## 5. Single Points of Failure — Summary

Three named single points of failure, ranked by valuation impact at Series C:

1. **Top 1 customer (22% of ARR)** — with a -8% renegotiation in flight and no documented loyalty mechanism beyond historical relationship. Loss or further compression of this account in the next 4 quarters would re-rate the company.
2. **Partner channel A (38% of Q1 new logos)** — currently broken. No diagnosed cause, no replacement plan. New logo velocity in Q2 2026 will be the disclosure point.
3. **Modeled vs measured NRR methodology** — the 115% figure propagated for two quarters without re-derivation. Until the finance function locks measured NRR as the source of truth, the next reporting cycle risks a repeat.
