# Worked Example: Revenue Story Audit

**Synthetic Co**: NorthStack — B2B fintech operations layer for mid-market neobanks. $20M ARR, Series B.

## Context

- Founded 2019. PMF achieved 2022. $20M ARR by Q1 2026.
- 47 FTEs.
- Series C kickoff scheduled in 90 days. CFO wants the revenue story to match the mechanics before it hits diligence.

## Symptom (Board-Deck-vs-Mechanics Gap)

- Board deck says "diversified revenue mix" — top 1 customer is 22% of revenue; top 10 are 64%.
- Board deck says outbound sales drives 60% of new ARR — segmented data shows outbound = 40%, inbound = 38%, partner referrals = 22%.
- Y/Y forecast in deck: +120%. Trailing 4-quarter actual growth: +47%.
- Leadership cites NRR at 115% ("bullish"). Last full-quarter actual NRR: 96%.
- 3 of top 10 customers in Q4 2025 had churn signals (usage decline, contract renegotiation, exec turnover) — not surfaced in the deck.
- One major partner referral source (38% of new logos in Q1 2026) went silent. Not flagged.

## Trigger Event

Series C kickoff in 90 days. CFO refuses to walk into the diligence room with a story she cannot defend line by line.

## Current Revenue Narrative (verbatim from last board deck)

> "NorthStack is executing against a multi-channel growth motion, with diversified revenue across customer segments and a sales-led GTM driving 60% of new ARR. The team is bullish on a +120% Y/Y trajectory, supported by 115% net revenue retention and durable expansion in the existing book."

## Evidence Available

- 4 quarters of revenue segmented by customer, channel, product, geography, cohort
- Last 2 board decks (Q3 2025, Q4 2025)
- Last sales pitch (deck + recorded call from Q1 2026)
- Top 10 deal expansions (last 4 quarters), with rep + reason + dollar amount
- Top 5 churn risks (last 2 quarters), with rep + reason + dollar amount
