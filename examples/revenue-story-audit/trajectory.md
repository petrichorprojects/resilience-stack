# Trajectory — Revenue Story Audit Facilitation Log: NorthStack

**Session date**: 2026-05-12
**Duration**: 2h 47m
**In the room**: CEO (Adrian), CFO (Priya), Head of Sales (Marcus). Head of CS (Naomi) joined at 1:08 by phone.
**Facilitator**: Petrichor Projects

---

## 0:00 — Phase 1 — Pre-Work Confirmation

Confirmed data inventory: 4 quarters segmented by customer + channel + product + geography + cohort, last 2 board decks, the Q1 2026 sales pitch, top 10 expansions, top 5 churn risks.

**Facilitator**: "Channel attribution — rep-coded or system-coded?"
**Marcus**: "Rep-coded."
**Facilitator**: "Then it's a story, not a measurement. We need system attribution before Segment 4."

Priya pulled marketing automation and product analytics during the first break. Reattribution shifted outbound from claimed 60% to actual 40%. Rep-coded attribution logged as an evidence integrity finding.

Read aloud the verbatim narrative from the Q4 2025 deck: *"NorthStack is executing against a multi-channel growth motion, with diversified revenue across customer segments and a sales-led GTM driving 60% of new ARR. The team is bullish on a +120% Y/Y trajectory, supported by 115% net revenue retention and durable expansion in the existing book."* Marcus laughed. Priya did not.

## 0:11 — Segment 1 — Revenue Forensics Dashboard Reveal

One slide, two columns: Claimed vs Measured. Diversified mix → Top 1 = 22%. Outbound 60% → 40%. +120% forecast → trailing 4Q +47%. NRR 115% → 96%.

**Adrian**: "Where did 115% come from?"
**Priya**: "Modeled, not measured. Q2 expansion run-rate annualized."
**Facilitator**: "You annualized a peak quarter without netting the contraction in the bottom decile."
Confirmed: NRR 115% was hopeful, not extrapolated.

## 0:26 — Segment 2 — The $1 Trace Exercise

Picked one dollar: a $185K compliance-module upsell from Customer #14. Traced origin to recognition. Origin: partner-referred lead from FinOps Network event. First touch: inbound webinar. CRM channel code: "Outbound" (rep manually re-coded after close).

**Facilitator**: "That dollar walked in through partner, converted on inbound, was outbound-stamped at close. Three of four channel signals on a single dollar are wrong."

Five other dollars traced. Same pattern on three. The 60% → 40% attribution gap now had a mechanism.

## 0:48 — Segment 3 — Board Deck Lie Detector

Walked the Q4 deck line by line, classifying each claim verified / extrapolated / hopeful / wrong.

- "Multi-channel growth motion" → verified
- "Diversified revenue across customer segments" → wrong (22% top-1)
- "60% outbound" → wrong (40% measured)
- "+120% Y/Y" → hopeful (linear extrapolation from one spike)
- "115% NRR" → wrong (96% measured)
- "Durable expansion in existing book" → extrapolated (3 of top 10 churn-signal)

12 claims audited; Priya admitted 4 were "extrapolated or hopeful — we never had the underlying data."

**Facilitator**: "Four out of twelve. Diligence finds it in week one. Better that you find it this morning."

## 1:08 — Segment 4 — Concentration Heat Map

Naomi joined. Pulled the concentration view: Top 1 = 22%, Top 2-10 = 42%, Top 11+ = 36%, top geo 71%, top product 64%.

**Adrian**: "I knew Top 10 was concentrated. I didn't know Top 1 was 22%."
**Facilitator**: "Series C buyer reads concentration as risk. 22% from a single customer with churn signals is a valuation hit you cannot un-fire."

Naomi flagged: Customer #1 renegotiated -8% effective price Q1 2026. Not in the deck.

## 1:24 — Break

## 1:36 — Segment 5 — Growth Assumption Autopsy

Walked the +120% forecast back to assumptions. Five named, each rated:

1. New logo velocity holds → wrong (partner channel silent since Feb).
2. Expansion ARR continues at Q2-Q3 run-rate → hopeful (3 of 10 churn-signal).
3. NRR holds at 115% → wrong (96%).
4. Outbound scales linearly with hires → unverified.
5. No price compression → wrong (Customer #1 -8%).

Rebuilt bottom-up from verified mechanics: **+60-75% Y/Y**. Forecast Integrity Score: **3/10**.

**Marcus**: "That number kills the round."
**Facilitator**: "That number gets the round done. The +120% number kills it in week two of diligence."

## 1:58 — Segment 6 — Anonymous Reveal

Four index cards. Each leader wrote the metric they hoped no one asked about. Read aloud by facilitator. Card 1: "NRR." Card 2: "NRR — the real one." Card 3: "Top-1 customer share." Card 4: "NRR."

Three of four wrote NRR. The team already knew. The deck hadn't caught up.

**Adrian**: "We've been managing the deck instead of managing the metric."

## 2:14 — Segment 7 — Reconciliation

Built both versions on the whiteboard.

- **Internal-Honest**: full mechanics, concentration disclosed, NRR 96% disclosed, forecast +60-75%, top-1 dependency flagged, partner channel broken disclosed, 3 churn-signal accounts named.
- **Externally-Defensible**: top 5 concentration disclosed (not top 1 alone), NRR trajectory (96% → 105% target with named path), revised forecast with assumptions and sensitivity bands, Top 11+ cohort durability emphasized (gross retention 94%).

**Facilitator**: "The Externally-Defensible version is not the spin version. It is Internal-Honest minus what fails diligence."

## 2:36 — Segment 8 — Score + Commitments

Composite Narrative Integrity Score: **4.1/10** pre-rebuild. Target: 7.5+ post-rebuild, gap <15%. Commitments locked to named owners with 14/30/60-day deadlines.

## 2:44 — Phase 3 — Deliverable Generation

Generated the 5-document package in-session.

**Adrian, closing**: "Three weeks ago I would have walked into diligence with the +120% number. I am genuinely relieved."
**Facilitator**: "Next skill: `investor-story-forensics` — pressure-test the reconciled story before the real diligence team shows up."

Session closed at 2:47. Deliverables shipped same day.
