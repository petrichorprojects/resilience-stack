# Post-Engagement Notes — NorthStack Revenue Story Audit

**Facilitator**: Petrichor Projects
**Session date**: 2026-05-12

---

## What Worked

The $1 Trace Exercise (Segment 2) did 60% of the diagnostic work in the first hour. Picking one dollar — the $185K compliance-module upsell from Customer #14 — and walking origin to recognition forced abstraction out of the channel attribution conversation. Once Marcus saw a partner-sourced, inbound-converted dollar carrying an "outbound" CRM code, the broader 20-point attribution gap was no longer arguable. It was mechanical. The room moved from defense to diagnosis in twelve minutes.

The Anonymous Reveal (Segment 6) was the second high-leverage moment. Three of four leaders wrote "NRR." The agreement was the diagnosis — the team already knew the 115% figure was unsound; the deck had simply not caught up. Adrian's line, "we've been managing the deck instead of managing the metric," became the operating frame for the back half.

Pre-work paid off. Priya pulled 4 quarters of segmented data ahead, which let the Concentration Heat Map land with a real Top-1 = 22% number rather than a hand-waved "we know we're concentrated." Team-supplied data could not be disputed.

## What Surprised

The rep-coded channel attribution finding surfaced during Phase 1, not in the planned Channel Truth segment. Priya disclosed in the first ten minutes that channel was self-reported at deal close — meaning the "60% outbound" claim was suspect before the audit formally opened. Move the channel attribution methodology question into Phase 1 as a hard prerequisite. If the answer is "rep-coded," halt and re-baseline before proceeding.

Head of CS (Naomi) was originally optional and joined late by phone. She immediately surfaced the Customer #1 -8% renegotiation — not in the deck, not in pre-work — which materially changed the concentration risk picture. For any company with >$5M ARR, Head of CS attendance must be mandatory from minute zero.

Adrian's closing line — "three weeks ago I would have walked into diligence with the +120% number, I am genuinely relieved" — confirmed Series C kickoff 90 days out was the correct moment for this skill.

## What To Do Differently

**Pre-pull cohort retention data.** The NRR decomposition in Segment 5 cost ~12 minutes because Priya had to pull live. For $10M+ ARR companies, cohort retention by quarter (Top 1, Top 2-10, Top 11+, last 8 quarters) should be a pre-work requirement.

**Build a partner-channel pre-flight check.** The FinOps Network silence was the most consequential finding and almost slipped through. For companies reporting >10% of revenue from partner sources, Phase 1 should include a 90-day activity check per partner. If a major partner has been silent >60 days, that is the lede, not a footnote.

Session ran 17 minutes long. Segment 5 took 22 minutes vs 15 budgeted because the team had not built the forecast bottoms-up before. Pre-circulate the assumption-decomposition worksheet 48 hours ahead so in-session time challenges assumptions rather than generates them.
