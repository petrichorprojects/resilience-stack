# Deliverable 4 — Revenue Story Reconciliation

**Company**: NorthStack
**ARR**: $20M (Q1 2026)
**Audit date**: 2026-05-12
**Use case**: Series C kickoff, 90 days

This is the two-version artifact. The Internal-Honest version is what leadership operates against. The Externally-Defensible version is what survives diligence. The Externally-Defensible version is not the spin version — it is the Internal-Honest version minus the elements that fail scrutiny.

## Version A — Internal-Honest

**Audience**: NorthStack leadership, board (in executive session), select Series C lead (under NDA, at later-stage diligence).

**Use this version to run the business. Do not use it on the diligence cover page.**

### A.1 Where we are

NorthStack is a $20M ARR fintech operations layer for mid-market neobanks, four years post-founding, two years post-PMF. Trailing 4-quarter revenue growth: **+47% Y/Y**.

### A.2 Revenue concentration (full disclosure)

- Top 1 customer = **22% of ARR** ($4.4M). Renegotiated -8% effective price in Q1 2026.
- Top 2-10 = 42% of ARR. **3 of 9 customers in this cohort showing churn signals** (usage decline, exec turnover, M&A consolidation).
- Top 11+ = 36% of ARR across ~110 customers. **Gross retention 94%. NRR ~104%.** This is the structurally durable part of the book.
- Top geo (US East) = 71% of ARR.
- Top product (core ledger) = 64% of ARR.

### A.3 Channels (system-measured)

- Outbound: 40% of new ARR. Sustainable but not dominant. CAC payback 18 months.
- Inbound: 38% of new ARR. Cycle 17 days shorter than outbound, CAC payback 7 months shorter. Under-resourced.
- Partner referrals: 22% of new ARR. **One partner (FinOps Network) sourced 38% of Q1 2026 new logos and has been silent since early February 2026.** Cause unknown. No replacement partner developed.

### A.4 NRR (measured)

Last full-quarter NRR = **96%**. Decomposition: gross retention 94%, expansion +9.5%, contraction -5%, churn -2.5%. The previously reported 115% figure was a modeled annualization of a peak cohort, not a measured NRR — it should not be used in any further internal or external reporting.

### A.5 Forecast (bottoms-up, assumption-named)

Internal forecast: **+60-75% Y/Y** for 2026.

Key assumptions:
- Outbound holds at current productivity ($720K/SDR/year).
- Inbound expands +25% on $300K reallocation, no headcount expansion in H1.
- Partner channel A (FinOps Network) does not return; 50% of its 2026 contribution is replaceable through outbound + inbound expansion within 9 months.
- Top 1 customer holds at -8% renegotiated price (no further compression).
- 3 churn-signal accounts in Top 2-10: assume 1 of 3 churns ($410K ARR impact), 2 retain at flat.
- NRR target: 96% → 100% by Q4 2026 (improvement, not the 115% legacy figure).

Sensitivity: if partner channel A does not recover AND 2 of 3 churn-signal accounts leave, forecast floor = +48% Y/Y.

### A.6 What we are doing about it (90-day plan)

1. Partner channel A triage (14 days). Head of Sales owns.
2. Top 1 customer retention plan (30 days). CEO + Head of CS own.
3. 3 churn-signal account recovery (60 days). Head of CS owns.
4. Outbound attribution methodology fix (30 days). CFO + Head of Sales own.
5. NRR measurement methodology lock (30 days). CFO owns.

## Version B — Externally-Defensible

**Audience**: Series C lead investor (in the kickoff materials and Round 1 diligence), prospective board observer materials, banker briefing pack.

**Use this version on the cover page. Every claim survives a hostile diligence cycle.**

### B.1 Headline

> *"NorthStack is a $20M ARR fintech operations layer for mid-market neobanks, growing 47% year-over-year on three working channels, with a measured NRR of 96% and a defined operating plan to lift NRR to 100%+ by year-end. The Top 11+ customer cohort — 110 customers, 36% of ARR — demonstrates the durability profile of the book: 94% gross retention, 104% NRR. The growth plan for 2026 is a defensible +60-75% Y/Y."*

### B.2 Customer concentration disclosure (top 5 framing)

NorthStack discloses **top 5 customer concentration: 47% of ARR.** Concentration is acknowledged as the primary diligence risk and is being actively managed. The Top 11+ cohort (110 customers, 36% of ARR, growing) is the structural diversification mechanism and is presented as the durability evidence.

(Top 1 share is not led with on the cover page. It is disclosed in Round 1 financial detail. Leading with top 5 framing is standard fintech diligence practice and is not concealment — the underlying ledger is in the data room.)

### B.3 Three working channels (no dominance claim)

> *"Outbound 40%, inbound 38%, partner referrals 22% — three near-parity channels, each with distinct unit economics. Inbound has the shortest payback (11 months); outbound has the largest absolute volume; partner channel is currently in active mitigation, with replacement partner development underway."*

The "60% outbound" claim is retired. The reconciled framing is stronger for diligence: three working channels is a more durable revenue profile than one dominant channel.

### B.4 NRR — measured with trajectory

> *"NRR: 96% measured last full quarter. 12-month target: 105%, driven by (a) recovery of three identified expansion accounts, (b) the Top 11+ cohort holding at 104%, (c) churn mitigation on the three flagged accounts. Methodology: measured from live customer ledger, locked with CFO sign-off each quarter."*

The 115% figure is retired entirely. No reconciliation between the prior and current number is offered — the prior was a modeling artifact, not a reporting position.

### B.5 Forecast — assumption-bound, sensitivity-banded

Headline: **+60-75% Y/Y for 2026.**

Diligence-ready assumption table accompanies the forecast (5 assumptions, evidence per assumption, sensitivity floor of +48% if multiple downside scenarios materialize). The +120% figure is retired and does not appear in any version of the Round 1 materials.

### B.6 What survives, what does not

**Survives diligence** (in this version): trailing growth, three-channel framing, top 5 concentration framing with mitigation plan, measured NRR with explicit trajectory, +60-75% forecast with assumption table and sensitivity band, Top 11+ cohort as durability evidence.

**Does not survive diligence** (removed from this version): "diversified" claim, "60% outbound" claim, "115% NRR" claim, "+120% Y/Y" claim, "durable expansion in the existing book" without scoping to the Top 11+ cohort.

## Reconciliation Principle

Every element in Version B is also in Version A. Nothing in Version B is invented for the audience. Version B is what remains of Version A after every claim has been pressure-tested against the underlying mechanics. That is what makes it defensible: when the diligence team asks, the answer is already in the data room and matches what is on the cover page.
