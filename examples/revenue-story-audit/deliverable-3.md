# Deliverable 3 — Channel Truth Assessment

**Company**: NorthStack
**ARR**: $20M (Q1 2026)
**Audit date**: 2026-05-12

This document compares NorthStack's claimed channel performance against system-measured attribution and recommends an action per channel: invest, sustain, fix, or kill.

---

## 1. Channel-by-Channel Truth Table

| Channel | Claimed share of new ARR | Measured share (TTM, system-attributed) | Status | Recommendation |
|---------|--------------------------|-----------------------------------------|--------|----------------|
| Outbound (SDR + AE-sourced) | 60% | **40%** | Over-credited | **Sustain** |
| Inbound (content + paid + organic) | (unspecified) | **38%** | Under-credited | **Invest** |
| Partner referrals | (unspecified) | **22%** | Currently broken | **Fix** |

The claimed/measured gap is not a measurement error. It is an attribution methodology error: channel was coded by the closing rep at deal close, not measured by the system at first-touch and conversion path. The fix is structural, not motivational.

---

## 2. Outbound — Claimed 60%, Actual 40%

**Root cause of over-credit**: Rep-coded channel at close defaults to "outbound" when an AE has any touch in the deal cycle, even when first-touch and the converting touch were inbound or partner-sourced. The $1 Trace Exercise found 3 of 6 traced dollars had been outbound-coded at close despite system data showing inbound or partner origin.

**Actual outbound contribution**:

- 40% of new ARR sourced (true outbound — SDR cold outreach, AE outbound prospecting, ABM-targeted accounts).
- Productivity per SDR FTE: $720K new ARR / year (12 SDRs, $8.6M outbound-sourced TTM).
- Cycle length: 71 days median.
- CAC payback: 18 months.

**Unit economics are sustainable, not exceptional.** Outbound is doing real work. It is not the dominant channel the deck claimed.

**Recommendation: Sustain.** Do not expand the SDR team in 2026. Do not contract it. Fix the attribution coding so future reports measure the channel honestly. Plan an outbound productivity review at the end of Q3 2026 to decide expansion.

---

## 3. Inbound — Claimed (Unspecified), Actual 38%

**Root cause of under-credit**: Inbound has been treated as "marketing support to outbound" in internal reporting. The system shows it as a near-peer channel to outbound by sourced ARR. The 38% figure includes content-sourced leads, paid-sourced leads, organic search, and event-driven inbound (webinars, podcast appearances).

**Actual inbound contribution**:

- 38% of new ARR sourced (content 19%, paid 11%, organic search 5%, events 3%).
- Productivity per marketing FTE: $1.4M new ARR / year (6 marketing FTEs, $8.2M inbound-sourced TTM).
- Cycle length: 54 days median (17 days shorter than outbound).
- CAC payback: 11 months (7 months shorter than outbound).

**Unit economics are stronger than outbound on every dimension measured.** Inbound has been under-resourced because it has been mis-credited.

**Recommendation: Invest.** Reallocate $300K of 2026 marketing budget from paid acquisition tests into content + organic infrastructure (the two highest-margin sub-channels). Hire one content marketer and one SEO specialist in H1 2026. Re-baseline channel attribution before any further reallocation.

---

## 4. Partner Referrals — Claimed (Unspecified), Actual 22% — Currently Broken

**Root cause of opacity**: Partner referrals were not broken out in the deck because the leadership team had not formally counted them. The number is 22% of TTM new ARR, sourced from 7 partner relationships, with one partner (FinOps Network) responsible for ~14 of those 22 points.

**Actual partner contribution**:

- 22% of new ARR sourced TTM, concentrated in 1 dominant partner (FinOps Network = ~63% of partner-sourced ARR).
- That dominant partner sourced 38% of Q1 2026 new logos.
- **Partner has been silent since early February 2026.** No new referrals in 90+ days.
- Cause: unknown as of audit date. Last contact: Jan 2026 monthly sync. No incident, no quarrel, no contract change.
- No backup partner has been developed in the FinOps Network niche.

**This is the single most fragile mechanic in the revenue stack.** The deck did not surface it. Diligence will find it the first time the buyer asks for partner-sourced revenue by partner.

**Recommendation: Fix — 14-day triage.** Head of Sales owns. Required actions: (a) direct outreach to FinOps Network exec contact within 7 days, (b) diagnose silence (relationship, incentive, competitive, internal change), (c) document mitigation plan if the channel does not recover, (d) identify 2 candidate replacement partners in the same niche by day 30. If the channel does not show signal of recovery within 60 days, treat it as gone in the reconciled forecast.

---

## 5. Channel Mix — Reconciled vs Claimed

| Framing | Outbound | Inbound | Partner |
|---------|----------|---------|---------|
| **Claimed (Q4 2025 deck)** | 60% (dominant) | implied secondary | not disclosed |
| **Reconciled (this audit)** | 40% (sustain) | 38% (invest) | 22% (fix, single-partner risk) |

The reconciled framing is both more honest and a stronger story for Series C. Three working channels at near-parity is a better diligence narrative than one dominant channel — provided the partner channel is either recovered or replaced before the diligence room.
