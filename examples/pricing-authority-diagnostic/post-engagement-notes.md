# Post-Engagement Notes — Helix Pricing Authority Diagnostic

**Facilitator**: Petrichor Projects
**Session date**: 2026-05-12

---

## What Worked

Pulling discount data by **rep** and by **manager-approval timestamp** before the session was the highest-leverage prep choice. In Segment 1, the quarter-end cluster after 4pm on the last 5 business days was visible without facilitator interpretation. Theo named it himself within 90 seconds. By Segment 2, the room had already accepted that "competitive pressure" was not the dominant story.

The Discount Autopsy (Segment 2) closed the rest of the gap. Walking the top 10 deals one by one, demanding the competing PDF in CRM for every "competitive" rep-code, recoded the distribution from 41% competitive / 22% buyer story / 18% annual / 12% champion to **38% quarter-end / 31% structural / 19% competitive / 12% other**. Reps had told these stories enough times that they sounded true; only deal-by-deal evidence broke them.

## What Surprised

The **per-provider value metric breaking at 100 providers was known to product 18 months ago and never escalated.** Yuki confirmed this in Segment 3 without prompting. Twelve customers and $4.2M ARR in the broken zone, absorbed by sales as a "tough negotiation" problem instead of escalated as a pricing-architecture problem. This is a cross-functional accountability gap, not just a pricing gap.

The board narrative ("discounts climbing due to competitive pressure, win rate stable") was structurally inverted. 69% of the discount problem is internal (rep behavior + product architecture). Win rate is stable only because Helix is buying it with discount.

## What To Do Differently

**Include Head of Customer Success from the start, not as optional.** Renata joined at 0:48 and within 8 minutes surfaced post-close interviews showing buyers had budget headroom in 4 of 5 audited deals. Without her, Deliverable 1 Section 4 (lost upside) would not have existed. For any NRR below 105%, CS attendance is mandatory from minute zero.

**Pre-pull NRR cohort data by initial tier and by provider-band.** Segment 6 ran ~14 minutes over because Renata pulled cohort retention live. For $20M+ ARR companies, cohort NRR by initial tier and by provider-band (<100, >100) should be pre-work.

**Add a "structural pricing issues known but not escalated" question to Phase 1.** Two minutes with Product would have surfaced the per-provider breakage during pre-work instead of mid-session.

Session closed 1 minute under budget. The rep-level discount pre-pull was the difference.
