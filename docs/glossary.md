# Glossary — Resilience Stack

Shared vocabulary across all 18 skills. Terms defined here override colloquial usage.

---

## A

**Admiralty Grid** — NATO 2-axis source-credibility framework (A-F × 1-6) adapted in `signal-vs-noise-audit` to grade market signals.

**Asymmetry Audit** — `competitive-blind-spot-mapping` exercise pairing competitor capabilities to our gaps and vice versa.

**Attack Surface** — In `competitive-narrative-stress-test`, the 5 narrative layers (Origin / Differentiation / Proof / Outcome / Future) where a competitor can land a hit.

## B

**BM25** — Sparse keyword-retrieval algorithm used to match skill descriptions to user tasks. Skills with concrete-task descriptions retrieve higher.

**Brand Permission** — The implicit agreement granted by market about what your brand is allowed to claim, expand into, or sell. Defined in `brand-permission-boundaries`.

## C

**Category Entry Point (CEP)** — A specific buying moment / trigger that surfaces a brand in buyer memory. From Romaniuk/Sharp, used in `brand-as-memory-system`.

**Composite Reward** — In SkillOS-style training, the multi-component reward (task outcome + format + compression + redundancy) used to train a Curator. Roadmap concept for v2.

**Composability** — A skill's ability to chain into other skills via shared deliverables. Tracked in frontmatter `composable_with`.

## D

**Decay Stage** — `relevancy-audit` classification: Drift (Stage 1) → Disconnect (Stage 2) → Displacement (Stage 3).

**Deliverable** — A structured artifact produced by a skill run, written to disk. Always 5 per skill in the Resilience Stack.

**Durability Score** — `positioning-under-pressure` composite output (0-100) measuring positioning resistance to 4 attack vectors.

## E

**Enemy Customer Interview** — Permission-to-be-honest customer interview used in `customer-truth-extraction` to break the politeness ceiling.

**Equity Draw-Down** — Cost in trust units of a brand extension. Calculated in `brand-permission-boundaries`.

**Evidence-Required** — A skill that refuses to run on speculation. Most Resilience Stack skills are evidence-required.

## F

**Familiarity Trap** — `false-familiarity` framework — buyers recognize the brand but cannot recall what it does at buying moment.

**Forensic Audit** — Document/data review that compares claims to mechanics. Used in `revenue-story-audit`, `investor-story-forensics`.

## H

**Heritage Asset** — A brand element earned over time (founder story, original product, original customer wins). Sorted into Transmission / Gifting / Retirement / Reinvention in `legacy-brand-relevance-reset`.

## J

**Jobs-To-Be-Done (JTBD)** — External framework (Christensen). Referenced but not native to Resilience Stack.

## P

**Permission Floor / Ceiling** — `brand-permission-boundaries` map: Floor = what market expects you to do (refusing costs trust); Ceiling = where extension breaks credibility.

**Pre-work** — Required evidence the user must gather before starting a skill. Listed in every skill's PHASE 1.

**Pressure Test Protocol** — `positioning-under-pressure` framework. 4 attack vectors: competitor displacement, market shift, narrative collapse, internal contradiction.

## R

**ReAct** — "Reasoning and Acting" agent loop pattern. Used by SkillOS Curator and referenced in roadmap.

**Relevancy Decay Model** — Petrichor's signature framework. Maps positioning decay across Drift → Disconnect → Displacement.

**Resilience Stack** — The repository: 18 strategy frameworks as Claude-runnable skills.

## S

**Signal Integrity Framework** — `signal-vs-noise-audit` framework. Combines Admiralty Grid + Response Bias Map + Decision Velocity Audit.

**Signal Refresh Roadmap** — `relevancy-audit` 90-day plan output: which signals to ship to refresh positioning.

**Skill Compass** — Meta-skill that selects the right Resilience Stack skill given symptom + stage + window + evidence.

**SkillOS** — Google paper (2026) framing skill management as an operating system w/ trainable Curator. Inspiration for v2 roadmap.

## T

**Telephone Audit** — `customer-truth-extraction` exercise where the value prop is passed through 5 customers; what comes back is what's actually believed.

**Tier (skill)** — 1 = Priority/Diagnostic, 2 = Strategic, 3 = Advanced/Stakeholder. Tracked in frontmatter.

**Track** — Skill domain: positioning / diagnostic / brand / growth / market-definition / intelligence / investor.

**Trajectory** — Full step-by-step record of a skill run. Used in v2 Curator training (roadmap).

## V

**Value Driver vs Value Story Gap** — `exit-narrative-architecture` measurement of distance between what creates value and what story emphasizes.
