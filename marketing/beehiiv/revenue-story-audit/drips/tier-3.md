## Email 2 — Day 4

**Subject:** What a Tier 3 reconciliation engagement looks like
**Preview:** 10 weeks. Three artifacts. The cost of waiting compounds weekly.

Hi {{first_name}},

Tier 3 engagement is structurally different from Tier 2 audit. The audit alone is not the deliverable. The engagement is audit + two-version reconciliation + rev-rec rules rebuild + SPOF mitigation plan + measurement plan. Ten weeks, not six.

**Weeks 1–2: forensic.**

All 9 kit segments plus three Tier 3 additions: full customer-level revenue rebuild from raw transaction data (not from CRM aggregations), full channel attribution rebuild from first-touch and multi-touch source data, written SPOF inventory with named owners and 90-day mitigation plans.

**Weeks 3–4: layer diagnosis.**

Output: a written diagnosis that picks one of three leading layers — data layer, narrative layer, attribution layer. Tier 3 engagements almost always involve at least two leading layers compounded by the third. The diagnosis names the leading layer, the lagging layer, and the dependencies.

**Weeks 5–6: rev-rec rules rebuild.**

If the leading layer is data, this is the work. New rev-rec rules covering expansion-vs-renewal-vs-new, customer-vs-account, motion classification, and revenue recognition timing. The rebuild gets applied to the trailing 12 months and the new numbers become the baseline.

**Weeks 7–8: two-version reconciliation construction.**

Internal-Honest + Externally-Defensible. Same shape as Tier 2 but with deeper appendix because the Tier 3 data has more reconstruction work behind it.

**Week 9: SPOF mitigation plan + surface rebuild.**

Named owners on each of the five SPOFs the audit surfaces. 90-day mitigation timelines. Deck rebuilt against the new baseline.

**Week 10: ship + measurement plan.**

You ship. 30-day measurement plan handed off.

Three artifacts you keep: rev-rec rules document, narrative brief, reconciliation appendix.

Fixed scope, fixed fee. Tier 3 engagements run materially higher than Tier 2 because the work is materially deeper. Fee is named on our first call after the layer diagnosis, not before.

## Email 3 — Day 7

**Subject:** A fintech that took the Tier 3 call 90 days before Series C
**Preview:** Forecast off by 120 points. Round closed at a 1.8x valuation cut. What changed.

Hi {{first_name}},

The synthetic case, drawn from a real engagement pattern (anonymized, composite).

Company: fintech infrastructure, $34M ARR, Series B + 22 months, 90 days from Series C kickoff. Quiz at engagement start: 14/15 Tier 3 Critical.

The picture at engagement start:

- Forecast vs trailing: deck +180% / trailing actual +60%. 120-point gap.
- Customer concentration: top-1 at 28%, top-3 at 58%, no named mitigation plans.
- Channel attribution: "we don't actually know — pipeline just shows up" was the literal answer from the head of sales.
- NRR: claimed 130% based on three expanding accounts. Cohort-anchored: 102%.
- SPOF inventory: none named.

The team had 90 days. The Petrichor engagement compressed to seven weeks (not the standard ten) because the timing demanded it. The compression cost two artifacts — we did not produce the full reconciliation appendix, and the channel attribution rebuild ran in parallel with the narrative brief rather than feeding it.

What changed by Series C kickoff:

- Forecast restated to trailing actuals. The deck went from +180% to +60% trailing, with the forecast forward at +45% (defensible under the rebuilt assumptions). The team had two leadership conversations with the board explaining the reset. The board saw the new baseline before the partners did.
- Customer concentration: top-1 mitigation plan named and executed. Top-1 percentage dropped to 24% by kickoff via one accelerated new logo close. Was not enough to drop below the 20% diligence line. The risk-factor footnote on the term sheet was anticipated, named in advance, and the mitigation timeline was on slide 4 of the deck.
- Channel attribution: not closed by kickoff. Was honest about it in the data room. The Externally-Defensible version cited "channel attribution rebuild in progress, methodology document attached as Appendix B."
- NRR: cohort-anchored 102% became the deck number. The 130% claim was retired.

The round closed. Valuation came in at a 1.8x cut from the team's original target — not because the reconciliation hurt the round, but because the trailing 47% growth had been overpriced in the original target. The reconciliation surfaced reality earlier than diligence would have, and the team negotiated the cut from a defensible position. If the reconciliation had happened during diligence rather than before, the cut would have been 2.5x or the round would have pulled.

The point of this case is not the cut. It is the timing. Catching Tier 3 90 days before kickoff is the difference between negotiating a cut and losing the round.

## Email 4 — Day 10

**Subject:** Three signals: Tier 3 vs severe Tier 2
**Preview:** The score is one input. Three other signals decide.

Hi {{first_name}},

Quiz score is one input. Three other signals separate a real Tier 3 from a severe Tier 2.

**Signal 1: SPOF inventory completeness.**

Tier 2: team can name two of three SPOFs without mitigation plans. Tier 3: team cannot name any SPOFs from memory, or names two and the audit surfaces three more. The gap between "what the team knows" and "what is actually there" is the Tier 3 signal.

**Signal 2: Forecast variance documentation.**

Tier 2: variance is large but each line is defensible with a specific assumption change written down somewhere. Tier 3: variance is large and the team cannot reconstruct why the assumptions changed. The forecast inherited assumptions from a window the team no longer remembers building.

**Signal 3: Channel attribution defensibility.**

Tier 2: head of sales can describe channel mix with a partial spreadsheet behind it. Tier 3: head of sales describes channel mix as "a mix" or "I don't actually know." Channel attribution at this level cannot be rebuilt in a one-month window. The engagement has to include a parallel attribution rebuild.

If you have all three signals, the engagement is Tier 3. If you have one or two, the engagement may still be Tier 2 with a tighter timeline. If you have none, you are at Tier 1 even if the quiz score was high — possible if a specific question hit you hard but the underlying mechanics are healthy.

The triage call decides which.

**Subject:** Who else to call if Petrichor is not the right fit
**Preview:** Three referrals. One question each.

Hi {{first_name}},

Last email. Some Tier 3 cases are not the right fit for Petrichor.

Most common reasons to refer out:

**1. The leading layer is data infrastructure, not narrative.**

When channel attribution has collapsed at the data infrastructure layer — not the framing layer — the work is a CDP rebuild and a rev-rec system review, not a narrative engagement. We refer to two data infrastructure partners. Question to ask each: "Can you produce a documented channel attribution methodology in 4 weeks against raw transaction data?" Most can do attribution. Few produce documented methodology that survives a QoE call. The methodology document is the asset.

**2. The leading layer is product (the revenue mix is unrecoverable without a roadmap change).**

When the trailing growth is concentrated in a product line the company is exiting, or when the SPOFs surface that two of the top three customers want a capability not on the roadmap, the work is product strategy, not narrative. We refer to one product strategy partner. Question to ask: "Can you produce a written product gap diagnosis in 4 weeks, with a specific roadmap-or-don't recommendation per gap?" The diagnosis is the asset.

**3. The timing is wrong for any engagement (you are inside 60 days of a partner meeting).**

If the round is already in motion and partners have seen the deck, the reconciliation work cannot complete before the diligence call. The right move at this stage is sometimes to pause the round, sometimes to negotiate a softer term sheet from a position of explicit honesty about the reconciliation work in progress. We refer to a fundraise advisor for this conversation, not a narrative partner. Question to ask: "Will you tell me to pause the round if pausing is the right move?" Some will. Most will not.
