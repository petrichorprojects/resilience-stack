The five things you scored on (win rate, sales cycle, buyer language, competitor surprise, share of voice) are the early indicators. They move two to three quarters before the board notices. ## Email 2 — Day 4

**Subject:** The 5 leading indicators of positioning drift
**Preview:** Watch these before the lagging metrics catch up.

Hi {{first_name}},

The diagnostic uses five signals because each one decays at a different speed and surfaces a different layer of the problem. Quick tour:

**1. Win rate trend.** Drifts first. A 3–15% drop is statistical noise to most teams. It is also the cheapest signal to ignore. The right question is not "is win rate down" — it is "is win rate down against the same competitor set in the same segment." A 10% drop against one specific competitor is a positioning problem. A 10% drop across the board is usually a market problem.

**2. Sales cycle length.** Drifts second. When buyers start needing more time to decide, the positioning has stopped doing pre-sales work. They are reaching the call already unsure what category to put you in.

**3. Buyer language match.** Drifts third — and this is the one that changes from "drift" to "disconnect." When buyers describe you in language you have never heard, the positioning has decoupled from how the market actually thinks. Check two places: discovery call transcripts and inbound demo requests.

**4. Competitor surprise frequency.** Drifts fourth. The first time a lost deal mentions a competitor your sales team has never heard of, your competitive intelligence has gone stale. The second time, your positioning is being attacked by a story you have never read.

**5. Share of voice in your category.** Drifts last and matters most. Once share of voice collapses, you are not in the category any more. You are in someone else's category, fighting for scraps.

Tomorrow: how to run the 30-minute version of the audit on yourself, no kit required.

— Phil

## Email 3 — Day 7

**Subject:** The 30-minute quarterly self-check
**Preview:** Five questions, three transcripts, one calendar reminder.

Hi {{first_name}},

The quarterly self-check is the difference between maintaining Stage 0 and discovering you have been in Stage 1 for nine months. It is 30 minutes. You do not need the full kit.

Block 30 minutes on the first day of each quarter. Bring the following:

- Last quarter's win rate by segment + competitor
- Last quarter's average sales cycle (in days)
- Three recent discovery call transcripts (any segment)
- Last three lost-deal post-mortems
- Your top three competitors' homepage hero copy (current, not last year)

Then ask the five quiz questions of yourself. Score honestly. If two signals show drift versus the previous quarter, you are not Stage 0 any more. You are Stage 1 Drift, and you have a 90-day window to fix it cheaply.

The discipline is not the score itself. It is the comparison to last quarter. The first run is a baseline. Every subsequent run is the data. After two quarters you have a trend line that surfaces drift before win rate does.

Two operational notes:

1. Run the check the same day each quarter. The point is the comparison. If the timing drifts, the data drifts.
2. Same person every time, or same panel. Different people score the same answers differently. Pick the most adversarial reader on your team.

## Email 4 — Day 10

**Subject:** How Synthetic Co caught Stage 1 at month 8
**Preview:** The signal that moved was not win rate.

Hi {{first_name}},

Synthetic Co (anonymized vertical SaaS, $30M ARR, Series B 11 months earlier) ran the quarterly self-check on month eight. Win rate had not yet moved. Sales cycle had not yet stretched. Share of voice was unchanged.

What moved was buyer language. Three discovery calls in a row, sales coded the buyer's words: "modern compliance tooling," "AI-augmented audit workflow," "policy automation." Synthetic Co's positioning was none of those. They sold "the compliance operating system for regulated industries." Word-for-word match: zero.

Their own internal language and their buyers' internal language had decoupled. The deck still worked. The website still worked. But the buyers were processing it through a translation layer they did not need to use.

The team ran the full audit in a 2.5-hour session. The output was a positioning refresh, not a re-platforming. They rebuilt the homepage hero in the buyer language they had been hearing in transcripts. They re-recorded the demo opening with the same words. They rewrote the first three slides of the sales deck.

Win rate moved 11 points in the following two quarters. The framework had not been broken. The translation layer had.

The point of the quarterly self-check is not to catch Stage 2 Disconnect. It is to catch Stage 1 Drift while the fix is still small. Synthetic Co caught it. Most companies do not.

## Email 5 — Day 14

**Subject:** When to escalate from self-check to full audit
**Preview:** The two triggers that move you out of maintenance mode.

Hi {{first_name}},

This is the last email in the welcome arc. The quarterly self-check covers 90% of Stage 0 maintenance. The remaining 10% — the part where the self-check stops being enough — has two triggers:

**Trigger 1: Two or more signals slip in the same quarter.**

One signal moving is noise. Two moving in the same direction inside one quarter is a pattern. When you hit this, the self-check has surfaced what it can. The full audit walks each of the nine segments in depth and produces a written positioning brief with claim-evidence anchors. That brief is the artifact you bring to the board, the next analyst briefing, or the next product planning cycle.

**Trigger 2: A reframe event — funding, a launch, a category challenger, leadership change.**

Stage 0 is calibrated against the market you were calibrated against. Any large event resets the calibration. A new round usually means new pressure on growth claims. A new launch usually changes which dimension you compete on. A new category challenger redraws share-of-voice math overnight. When any of these happen, the maintenance posture stops fitting. Run the full audit within 60 days of the event, not after.

If neither trigger fires for the next 12 months, you do not need to talk to Petrichor. Keep running the quarterly self-check. Use the kit when you want to ladder up to a full diagnostic.
