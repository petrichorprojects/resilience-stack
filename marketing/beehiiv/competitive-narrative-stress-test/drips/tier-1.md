## Email 1 — Day 2

**Subject:** Kit + the 9 stress-test segments, in order
**Preview:** Open it Monday. The stress test works the same week.

Hi {{first_name}},

Tier 1 Fragile is the cheapest stage to harden. One or two attack surfaces have started cracking. The pattern is not yet visible in the boardroom — win rate is wobbling, sales has stopped reading competitor decks in full, the outcome number on slide 12 has lost its methodology trail. You have 60–90 days to reset before the next analyst cycle or fundraise window calibrates the standard.

The kit — [competitive-narrative-stress-test kit](https://github.com/petrichorprojects/resilience-stack/tree/main/skills/positioning/competitive-narrative-stress-test). Block 2.5 hours. Bring CEO, head of sales, head of product. The stress test does not work without all three because the cracks at Tier 1 usually live between functional views of the same competitive position.

The 9 segments, in order:

1. Win rate decomposition (by competitor and segment)
2. Lost-deal post-mortem coding (last 10 losses, verbatim)
3. Competitor verbatim pull (top 3 decks, word-for-word, not summarized)
4. Future-claim survivability audit (every future-state claim in current deck)
5. Outcome methodology audit (every numbered claim, traced to evidence)
6. Analyst language delta (last 12 months of category coverage)
7. 60-Second Delivery Test (3 executives, recorded, scored)
8. Buyer language match against current narrative (3 transcripts minimum)
9. Internal language consistency (CEO vs sales vs marketing vs product)

Each segment takes 15–20 minutes if inputs are pre-staged. The kit's first page is the inputs checklist. Pre-stage before the session. Teams that skip lose the first hour reconstructing data.

## Email 2 — Day 4

**Subject:** How to run the Blank Check Test in 30 minutes
**Preview:** Assume a competitor has unlimited budget. Where do they attack first?

Hi {{first_name}},

The Blank Check Test is the single most diagnostic exercise in the stress-test kit. It is also the exercise teams underestimate because it looks like a thought experiment and it is not.

The protocol, 30 minutes:

**Minutes 0–10: setup.** Pull the three competitors most likely to attack your narrative in the next 12 months. Not the three biggest competitors. The three most likely to attack — the ones with the capability, the budget, and the strategic reason to invest in narrative warfare against you.

For each, write one sentence: "If [competitor] had a blank check and 12 months, the cheapest place to attack our narrative would be [attack vector]."

The attack vector is one of the five surfaces — win rate (capture share in one segment), competitor verbatim (publish a side-by-side that we cannot answer), future claims (ship the capability we have been promising), outcome methodology (publish a customer study that beats ours on math), or 60-second delivery (release a tagline so good our CEO starts using it accidentally).

**Minutes 10–25: red team.** Write the attack. Not a summary — the actual artifact. The press release, the comparison page, the customer case study, the tagline. Write it as if you worked for the competitor. Three to five paragraphs per attack vector. Be specific. The press release names a customer. The comparison page has actual columns. The case study cites a number.

**Minutes 25–30: defense audit.** For each written attack, ask one question: do we have a defensible response in the next 90 days? Yes / no / partial.

The pattern across three competitors and five surfaces is the diagnostic. If two of three attacks land on the same surface, that surface is your softest. If a defensible response is "no" on more than two attacks, the narrative is past Tier 1 — you are in Tier 2 Cracking territory.

## Email 3 — Day 7

**Subject:** The 60-Second Delivery Test — what hostile questioning sounds like
**Preview:** Three executives. Sixty seconds. Adversarial follow-up.

Hi {{first_name}},

The 60-Second Delivery Test is mechanical. Three executives, each delivers the competitive narrative cold, in 60 seconds, with one round of adversarial follow-up.

**Setup:**

Pick three executives — CEO, head of sales, head of product. Schedule three 10-minute sessions. Each executive delivers the narrative independently, recorded.

**Round 1: cold delivery (60 seconds).**

Prompt: "An analyst from Gartner just stopped you in a hallway. You have 60 seconds to convince her your product belongs in the Leaders quadrant. Go."

Score:

- Convergence: do the three deliveries use the same words for the same concepts?
- Sequence: do they surface the same claims in roughly the same order?
- Proof-point density: are they citing specific proof points or general claims?

**Round 2: adversarial follow-up (60 seconds).**

After the 60-second delivery, ask one hostile follow-up. Pick the follow-up based on which attack surface scored weakest in the Blank Check Test.

- If outcome methodology is weak: "Walk me through the math on the $12M saved claim."
- If future-claim survivability is weak: "How exactly do you plan to deliver decisions-grade analytics in 12 months?"
- If competitor verbatim is weak: "How is this different from [competitor]'s positioning?"

Score the follow-up on three dimensions: specificity, defensibility, and convergence with the cold delivery.

**The diagnostic:**

A narrative where three executives diverge on the cold delivery is rehearsable. Three rehearsals close it.

A narrative where three executives converge on the cold delivery but diverge on the adversarial follow-up is the Tier 1 pattern. The team knows the talking points. The team does not own the proof points. The reconstruction work is to attach proof points to talking points.

A narrative where three executives diverge on both is past Tier 1 — Tier 2 Cracking. Reconstruction requires more than rehearsal; it requires deciding which claims are load-bearing and which are decoration.

## Email 4 — Day 10

**Subject:** How Tessera caught Tier 1 fragility 90 days before Series B
**Preview:** Attack surface 13/25 → 19/25 in 2.5 hours.

Hi {{first_name}},

Tessera (anonymized retail analytics SaaS, $14M ARR) ran the full stress test 90 days before kickoff on a Series B raise. Tier 1 going in. Win rate had dropped from 52% to 31% over two quarters. Sales cycle had stretched. Four of the seven losses in the trailing quarter cited a specific competitor as the deciding factor.

The stress-test scorecard at engagement start: 13/25 across the five surfaces. The Blank Check Test surfaced two of three likely competitor attacks landing on outcome methodology. The 60-Second Delivery Test surfaced convergence on the cold delivery, divergence on adversarial follow-up.

Three actions in the 2.5-hour session:

**Action 1: Outcome methodology rebuild.** The strongest customer-outcome claim — "$340M in shelf-decision lift across 12 retail customers" — had been published in marketing copy without supporting methodology. The team pulled the underlying customer studies, documented the methodology in a four-page appendix, and published it as a downloadable on the customer-evidence page. The claim went from folklore to public-methodology in one working week.

**Action 2: Future-claim proof points.** The deck's strongest future-state claim — "real-time decisions at retail-shelf scale" — had a 12-month roadmap but no proof-point bridge. The team built a three-point bridge using one capability already shipped, one capability in beta, and one capability with an executed engineering plan and a named owner. The bridge became slide 8 of the new deck.

**Action 3: 60-Second Delivery rehearsal.** Three executives rehearsed the cold delivery and the adversarial follow-up four times each across two days. The convergence and proof-point density both improved measurably in the recordings.

Post-reconstruction scorecard: 19/25. Three of the five surfaces moved up.

The Series B kickoff three months later: pipeline mix was healthier, win rate had recovered to 44% in the trailing two months, and the Gartner briefing that took place mid-fundraise resulted in coverage that explicitly cited the new outcome methodology.

The point is not the win rate recovery. It is the timing. Catching Tier 1 fragility 90 days before kickoff was the difference between a Series B raise from a defensible competitive position and a Series B raise where partners would have asked the same questions Tessera was now ready to answer.

## Email 5 — Day 14

**Subject:** How to tell whether your reconstruction actually improved durability
**Preview:** The 30-day re-test, with one Tier 1-specific check.

Hi {{first_name}},

Last email in the welcome arc.

The reconstruction feels right in the room. The deck got tighter. The team aligned on the new framing. Two months later the same fragility reopens because the underlying maintenance cadence never got set up.

Re-test protocol, 30 days after reconstruction:

**1. Same five questions, same scoring.** Quiz form: [tally.so/r/jaJg86](https://tally.so/r/jaJg86). The point is the comparison.

**2. Same data inputs, fresh window.** Win rate trend from the 30 days after, not the week of. Buyer language transcripts from new discovery calls. Competitor decks pulled in the last 30 days.

**3. Score honestly.** Two or more surfaces improved = the reconstruction worked. Tier 1 Fragile is closed. Resume the quarterly self-check.

**4. Tier 1-specific check.** Re-run the 60-Second Delivery Test with the same three executives. Score convergence and proof-point density. If both improved, the narrative has been internalized. If convergence improved but proof-point density did not, the team learned the script without internalizing the evidence — a temporary fix that erodes in 60–90 days.

**5. If less than two surfaces improved.** Two possibilities:

- The team rebuilt the deck without rebuilding the operational discipline. Competitor verbatim discipline requires a 30-day cadence. Outcome methodology requires public documentation. 60-Second Delivery requires regular rehearsal. The deck can be rebuilt in a session; the disciplines have to be installed. Diagnostic: did the reconstruction install new cadences, or just produce a new artifact?
- The fragility was at a deeper layer than the stress test addressed. If your category is bifurcating or your product roadmap is lagging the market, narrative work alone cannot close the gap. The full audit (Relevancy Decay framework) is the next instrument.

If less than two improved, escalate. Reply to this email or book — {{calendly-url}}.

Otherwise: quarterly self-check stays on the calendar. Hear from me when I have something specific worth your inbox.
