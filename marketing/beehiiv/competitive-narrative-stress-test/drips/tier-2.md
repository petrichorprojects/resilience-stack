## Email 2 — Day 4

**Subject:** Claim-evidence anchoring: the framework that closes Tier 2 cracks
**Preview:** Every claim with a citation. Every citation with a method.

Hi {{first_name}},

The biggest mistake teams make at Tier 2 is treating the reconstruction as a re-write. It is not. A Tier 2 narrative does not crack because the words are wrong. It cracks because the words are no longer attached to evidence the buyer can verify.

Claim-evidence anchoring is the framework. Three layers.

**Layer 1: Claim catalog.**

List every claim in your current narrative. Every line in the deck. Every paragraph on the homepage. Every sentence in the one-liner. Each claim is one row.

For each claim, code three dimensions:

- Type: capability (what we do) / outcome (what customers get) / category (what we are) / future (what we will do).
- Weight: load-bearing (the narrative dies without this) / supporting (the narrative wobbles without this) / decoration (the narrative survives without this).
- Current evidence: cited / partial / folklore / none.

The output is one spreadsheet with 40–80 rows. Most teams have never built this.

**Layer 2: Evidence inventory.**

For every load-bearing claim, build the evidence trail. The trail has three properties:

- Source: customer interviews, product analytics, sales data, third-party research, published case study.
- Method: how the source was processed into the claim (sample size, methodology, time window, scope).
- Defensibility: would the evidence survive an analyst's reconstruction call without your team in the room?

Any load-bearing claim with folklore evidence is a hard flag. Any load-bearing claim with no evidence is a kill — either build the evidence in the next 60 days or remove the claim from the narrative.

**Layer 3: Public surface.**

The reconstructed narrative has every load-bearing claim anchored to public evidence the buyer can find without talking to your team. The case study with methodology. The downloadable benchmark. The customer interview with named source. The published roadmap with proof points.

The public surface is the moat. A competitor who reads your narrative cannot defeat you on a claim that has public methodology behind it. They can only defeat you on claims with folklore evidence.

## Email 3 — Day 7

**Subject:** Tessera's Tier 2 scorecard and the three 30-day actions
**Preview:** 13/25. Win rate 52→31%. Five months pre-Series B.

Hi {{first_name}},

Tessera at the start of the engagement: $14M ARR retail analytics SaaS, five months from Series B kickoff. Win rate 52% → 31% across two quarters. Quiz composite: 10/15 Tier 2 Cracking.

The full stress-test scorecard:

| Surface | Score (0–5) | Read |
|---|---|---|
| Win rate trend | 1 | -21 points across two quarters, concentrated against one competitor |
| Competitor verbatim discipline | 2 | Last competitor deck read 78 days earlier |
| Future-claim survivability | 1 | "Real-time decisions" claim, no proof-point bridge |
| Outcome methodology | 1 | "$340M lift" claim, folklore evidence |
| 60-Second Delivery | 3 | Convergence good, adversarial follow-up weak |
| (audit segments 6-9 added) | 5/20 | Stress test segments below surface 5 |

Total: 13/25. The proof-layer surfaces (3, 4) were the lowest. The discipline-layer surface (2) was second-lowest. The 60-Second Delivery surface scored well on cold delivery and poorly on adversarial follow-up — the team had memorized the script without internalizing the proof.

Three 30-day actions, in priority order:

**Action 1: Outcome methodology rebuild.** Two senior team members + one analyst, four working days. The team pulled the underlying customer studies for the "$340M lift" claim. Methodology was written up — sample size (12 retail customers across 4 quarters), measurement window (90-day pre/post comparison), control methodology (matched-market design where available, paired-store design where not), and limitations (selection bias acknowledged, magnitude estimate). The methodology document was 6 pages. It was published on the customer-evidence page as a downloadable PDF with the marketing claim.

**Action 2: Future-claim proof-point bridge.** Head of product + head of marketing, three working days. The "real-time decisions" claim was decomposed. The bridge: one capability already shipped (sub-second query response on shelf data), one capability in beta with three customers (decision recommendation engine), one capability with executed engineering plan and named owner (automated decision execution for Q3 launch). The three-point bridge became slide 8 of the new deck.

**Action 3: Competitor verbatim discipline reset.** Head of product marketing, recurring cadence. New process: top 3 competitor decks pulled and read word-for-word on the first Monday of each month. Summary memo written. Briefing to leadership in the second-Monday weekly meeting. The cadence was set, not just promised — a recurring calendar invite and a recurring deliverable.

Post-reconstruction scorecard at week 6: 19/25. Series B kickoff three months later proceeded from a defensible competitive position. Round closed clean.

## Email 4 — Day 10

**Subject:** What a Petrichor engagement looks like end-to-end
**Preview:** Six weeks, three artifacts, one defensible narrative.

Hi {{first_name}},

The full engagement shape, no marketing veneer.

**Week 1: data assembly.**

You provide: 4 quarters of win/loss data with competitor coding, 10 most recent lost-deal post-mortem transcripts (verbatim), current deck + homepage + one-pager, top 5 competitor decks (verbatim or links), last 12 months of analyst category coverage, current customer-outcome claims with whatever evidence exists today. We audit the data within 72 hours and flag gaps. We do not start segment work until inputs are clean.

**Week 2: full stress test.**

We run all 9 segments in two 4-hour working sessions with CEO, head of sales, head of product. Output: scorecard, layer diagnosis (proof layer / discipline layer / positioning layer), written hypothesis brief naming the leading layer.

**Weeks 3–4: claim-evidence reconstruction.**

We build the claim catalog (40–80 rows) and the evidence inventory. Every load-bearing claim either gets an evidence trail or gets killed. Drafts go to CEO, head of sales, head of product, head of marketing.

**Week 5: surface rebuild.**

Homepage hero, deck opening, demo opening, one-pager. The public-evidence pages (methodology PDFs, customer case studies, future-claim proof bridges). Your team builds the sales enablement and analyst briefing kits with the brief as input.

**Week 6: ship + measurement plan.**

You ship. We hand off a 30-day measurement plan against the five quiz surfaces plus three Tier 2-specific signals (deck-page time, methodology PDF downloads, competitor-mention frequency in lost deals). You re-test in 30 days.

Three artifacts you keep: claim catalog, evidence inventory, competitive narrative brief.

Fixed scope, fixed fee. Tier 2 proof-layer engagements run roughly $55k. Discipline-layer engagements run roughly $45k. Positioning-layer engagements ladder up into a fuller relevancy audit and the fee is calibrated on the call.

## Email 5 — Day 14

**Subject:** What to bring to the triage call
**Preview:** Scorecard, three lost-deal stories, the contested claim.

Hi {{first_name}},

Last email. If we are going to talk, the triage call is the next step. {{calendly-url}}.

Bring three things.

**1. The stress-test scorecard.**

Filled in. All 9 segments scored. Total at the bottom. Without it we spend 20 of the 30 minutes reconstructing the scorecard. With it we go straight to the layer diagnosis.

**2. Three lost-deal stories.**

Not the post-mortem documents. The story in three sentences each. Who did you lose to. What was the buyer's stated reason. What was the surface where your narrative failed (proof / future-claim / methodology / delivery). The pattern across three losses tells me where the narrative is bleeding faster than the surface diagnostic surfaced.

**3. The claim your leadership team disagrees about.**

If your team is unanimous on the current narrative, you are not at Tier 2 Cracking — you are at Tier 1 or 0. The disagreement is the diagnostic. Bring the most contested line — the deck claim, the homepage hero, the one-liner. We will read the disagreement together.

The call ends with one of three answers (internal reconstruction / Petrichor engagement / different team).
