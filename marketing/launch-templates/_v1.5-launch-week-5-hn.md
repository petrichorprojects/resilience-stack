## Cumulative Recap LinkedIn Post (post Tuesday after HN goes live)

**Timing:** Tuesday 2026-06-17, ~11:00 AM ET (2 hours after HN post, enough time for early comments to surface). Post natively on LinkedIn — do not link out as the primary CTA.

**Subject:** Six weeks. One manifesto. Five polished kits. What we learned.

**Body (~1200 words):**

Six weeks ago I shipped the Resilience Stack manifesto. The thesis was one sentence: strategy frameworks should be audit-and-decay tools, not playbooks, because the most expensive failures in early-stage companies happen when the team mistakes a framework that worked once for a framework that still works. Decks decay. Positioning decays. Pricing rationales decay. Competitive narratives decay. Investor stories decay. The thing that needs to be operationalized is not the framework — it is the discipline of running the audit before the gap becomes a board meeting.

Today the full v1.5 release is live. One manifesto plus five polished kits. Eighteen total skills in the repo, five of them with worked examples, sample deliverables, and evals. Each kit ships as a single markdown file that runs in any modern AI assistant — Claude, the Anthropic Skills format, or anything that respects the structure. CC BY 4.0 on the marketing artifacts, MIT on the code. No paid tier. No gated PDFs.

Here is what the five kits cover and what each one is for.

**Kit 1 — relevancy-audit.** Released Week 1. Diagnoses whether a company's positioning still matches how buyers actually evaluate the category. The most common entry point for teams new to the repo because it produces the clearest before-and-after scorecard. The worked example shipped with a 12/25 Cracking score lifted to 19/25 Holding after a 2.5-hour facilitation. This is the kit to run quarterly between fundraises.

**Kit 2 — pricing-authority-diagnostic.** Released Week 2. Surfaces the gap between the willingness-to-pay your pricing page implies and the willingness-to-pay buyers actually signal in conversation. Most companies are pricing against a category that has moved without them. The kit forces the team to read three months of sales calls verbatim and score the WTP signal against the public pricing claim.

**Kit 3 — competitive-narrative-stress-test.** Released Week 3. Five attack surfaces — Origin, Differentiation, Proof, Outcome, Future. Each scored 1-5 for attack resistance. Composite Durability 5-25. The audit runs three pressure mechanisms in sequence: the Blank Check Test, the Competitor's Best Day Simulation, and the 60-Second Delivery Test. The kit that gets the most inbound from operators 6-12 months from a Series B.

**Kit 4 — belief-stress-test.** Released Week 4. Pressure-tests the founder's own thesis. Most founders have never read the bear case a sophisticated investor would write against them. This kit produces it. It is the kit operators run reluctantly, usually after one of the other four has surfaced a gap they cannot self-audit.

**Kit 5 — investor-story-forensics.** Released Monday. The diagnostic that catches the gap between the pitched investor narrative and the auditable narrative — eight to fourteen months before the partner meetings start. Scores every load-bearing claim 0-3 against trailing data. Composite Narrative Integrity 0-30. The Aperture worked example walked through a 14/30 Vulnerable scorecard lifted to 21/30 Defensible.

**What the signal looks like after five weeks.**

The quizzes have produced more usable inbound than the essays, by a meaningful multiple. Operators self-routing into the Vulnerable and Cracking tiers and then booking calls without prompting. The Cracking and Collapsed tiers convert at roughly 30 percent into a real conversation. The Holding and Defensible tiers convert at roughly two percent, which is correct — those tiers do not need a call.

The repo stars are a vanity metric. The metric that actually mattered through the launch was the share of inbound emails that quoted a specific scorecard line back at me. Roughly one in three. That is the signal the framework vocabulary is sticking — operators are now describing their own situation using "Layer 5 Future Claim" and "Narrative Integrity 14/30" without prompting. The kit has become diagnostic vocabulary for a subset of the audience.

**What I would do differently.**

Three things.

One — I would have shipped the quizzes a week earlier than the essays. The quiz is the highest-converting asset in every kit. The essay is the highest-trust asset but the slowest. Inverting the sequence by a week would have routed more operators into the right tier before they read the long-form.

Two — I would have shipped a single composite scorecard across all five kits in the v1.5 release, not just per-kit scorecards. The most common inbound question after Week 3 was "can I run all five against my company?" The answer was yes, manually. The v2 release will produce a single Resilience Score across positioning, pricing, competitive, belief, and investor — one number, five anchors.

Three — I would have written the limits sections of each skill earlier. The "When NOT to use" block was the highest-trust paragraph in every essay. Operators trust a framework that names what it does not do. Most strategy content does not do this, which is why most strategy content does not get used twice.

**What is next.**

v1.6 over the summer — three additional kits in the pipeline: founder-bear-case, board-narrative-stress-test, customer-truth-extraction. v2 in the fall — the composite Resilience Score and a structured integration of all eight kits into a single audit flow. The repo will stay free. The Petrichor engagement will stay scoped to operators who have already run the relevant kit and want the audit run in the room with them.

**The repo.**

github.com/petrichor-projects/resilience-stack

The entry point for most teams is the relevancy-audit kit. The quizzes for all five kits are linked from each kit's README. The manifesto is at docs/manifesto.md. CC BY 4.0 on everything I wrote.

Thanks to everyone who took a quiz, opened a kit, sent a teardown of a scorecard, or emailed me a Tier 3 result. The work this fall is built on what those notes surfaced.
