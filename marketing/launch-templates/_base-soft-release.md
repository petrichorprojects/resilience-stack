## Post copy

**Every framework in your strategy deck is decaying. The discipline of noticing the decay is the only durable strategic moat left.**

That sentence is the thesis of an essay I published last week called *Why Frameworks Decay*. It is also the reason we built Resilience Stack.

Resilience Stack is an open-source library of strategy diagnostic frameworks, packaged as Claude-runnable skills. v1.2 is live today — 18 skills across positioning, pricing, ICP, category narrative, sales motion, and operating principles. Each skill is a structured diagnostic: 9 facilitation segments, a worked example, 5 sample deliverables, an evaluation suite, and a scoring rubric. License is CC BY 4.0. Take it, fork it, run it on your business.

The Petrichor thesis is simple: most strategy work fails not because the frameworks are wrong, but because they were calibrated to a market that has since moved 5–30% away from the assumptions baked in. A framework with a freshness date is a tool. A framework without one is a fossil with a logo on it.

Over the next 5 weeks I'm publishing one polished kit per week. Each kit will land Monday morning with the skill, a diagnostic quiz, a Loom walkthrough, and a long-form essay explaining when the framework works and — more importantly — when it does not. Week 5 closes with a Show HN.

If you run strategy inside a company, advise companies that do, or just want to see what a maintained library of strategy frameworks looks like when you treat the maintenance as the work — bookmark the repo.

**Repo:** https://github.com/petrichorprojects/resilience-stack

*— Phil, Petrichor Projects*
