### Shot 3 — DEMO (90 seconds)

**Camera:** screen-focused. Right half (Claude Code) becomes the focus.
**Visual cues:** open `examples/pricing-authority-diagnostic/input.md`. Copy the full contents. Paste into Claude Code with the pricing-authority-diagnostic skill loaded. Hit enter. Let the response stream live. Point to the screen as each segment appears.

**Narration (verbatim):**

"This is the worked example. Helix. Healthcare scheduling SaaS for outpatient clinics. Twenty-eight million ARR. Discount percentage climbed from eight to twenty-one over the last twelve months. NRR moved from one hundred eight down to ninety-six. Thirty months without a price increase. Expansion stuck at eight percent against a target of twenty-five. I am pasting their full intake into Claude Code right now. The skill is loaded. Watch what happens. Claude does not jump to a recommendation. The first thing it does is confirm the data inventory — every closed-won deal in the last four quarters, list price, sold price, discount percent, discount reason coded by rep, top ten discounts ready for recoding. Now it runs the Discount Autopsy. Take the ten largest discounts last quarter. Recode the reason. The rep field on every one of them says 'competitive pressure' or 'buyer pushback.' The recoded reasons are different. Thirty-eight percent of the discount volume was quarter-end manager-approved with no competitive pressure documented in the CRM. Thirty-one percent was structural — the per-provider value metric breaks above one hundred providers and discount is the workaround. Nineteen percent was actual competition. Twelve percent was other. The buyer story dies in the recoding. Then the Value Metric Stress Test. Per-provider. Does the buyer have a way to verify the count? Yes. Does the per-unit price still hold when the buyer has two hundred providers? No — clinics above one hundred are systematically discounted because the metric does not scale. This is what the skill does. It produces a sequence of findings, each one defensible, each one referenced to evidence in the intake."

### Shot 5 — SCORECARD WALKTHROUGH (45 seconds)

**Camera:** screen-focused. Right half shows `examples/pricing-authority-diagnostic/deliverable-5-scorecard.md`.
**Visual cues:** open the file. Show the composite score (18 / 30 ERODING). Scroll to the top 3 actions. Pause on the leading indicator at the bottom.

**Narration (verbatim):**

"This is the scorecard. Composite Pricing Authority Score eighteen out of thirty. ERODING — target twenty-four out of thirty. Three actions for the next ninety days. Redesign the value metric — replace flat per-provider with a tiered usage-based component above one hundred providers, owner CEO and head of product, deadline forty-five days. Tighten the discount-approval ladder — manager approval cap moved from twenty-five percent to fifteen percent, anything above goes to CFO with a written competitive justification, owner CFO and VP Sales, deadline fourteen days. Run a 10% list-price increase on net-new Enterprise contracts in the next quarter, owner CEO, deadline sixty days. Leading indicator at the bottom — monthly average discount percentage on net-new deals. When that number drops below twelve percent and holds for three months, the discipline is back. The diagnostic takes two and a half hours. The scorecard is what the operator hands to someone else on a Wednesday morning and says 'do these three things by these dates.' That is the entire point of the kit."
