## 6 Shots

### Shot 1 — HOOK (30 seconds)

**Camera:** webcam full-frame for this shot. Screen visible behind but not the focus.
**Visual cues:** founder talking head. No slide. No title card. Just face on camera.

**Narration (verbatim):**

"Your win rate dropped twelve percent in two quarters. Your sales cycle stretched from forty-seven days to sixty-two. Three lost deals last quarter named the same competitor by name, and your sales team had never heard of them. Nothing else changed. Same product. Same team. Same pricing. Same playbook. This is what positioning decay looks like before anyone in the company is willing to call it that. The relevancy-audit kit is the diagnostic that catches it. Five minutes. Let me show you what it does."

### Shot 2 — SKILL STRUCTURE (45 seconds)

**Camera:** screen-focused. Webcam shrinks to corner. Left half (skill markdown) is the focus.
**Visual cues:** scroll through `skills/positioning/relevancy-audit/relevancy-audit.md`. Pause on the frontmatter. Pause on the "When NOT to use" block. Pause on the 9 facilitation segments.

**Narration (verbatim):**

"The skill file is one markdown document. Top of the file is the frontmatter — name, version, when to use it, when not to use it. The 'when not to use' block is the part I want you to read. It says: do not run this if you have less than four quarters of win-loss data, do not run this if your win rate change is under three percent, and do not run this if you have not achieved product-market fit yet. That block exists because the most expensive mistake operators make with this framework is running it against signal that is too noisy to act on. The skill says no for you, so you do not have to. Below that, nine facilitation segments. Customer Amnesia Test. Buyer Language Audit. Competitor Surprise Index. Decay Stage Classification. Each segment is one question, run in order, producing one artifact. The order matters. The diagnostic does not work if you skip ahead."

---

### Shot 3 — DEMO (90 seconds)

**Camera:** screen-focused. Right half (Claude Code) becomes the focus.
**Visual cues:** open `examples/relevancy-audit/input.md`. Copy the full contents. Paste into Claude Code with the relevancy-audit skill loaded. Hit enter. Let the response stream live. Point to the screen as each segment appears.

**Narration (verbatim):**

"This is the worked example. Synthetic Co. Thirty million ARR. Vertical SaaS for legal operations. Plateaued in 2024. Win rate dropped from thirty-eight percent to twenty-four. I am pasting their full intake into Claude Code right now. The skill is loaded. Watch what happens. Claude does not jump to a recommendation. The first thing it does is confirm the data inventory — what is here, what is missing. Synthetic Co has four quarters of win-loss, eighteen customer surveys with verbatim NPS, three competitor positioning statements, and a Q4 analyst quadrant. What is missing is lapsed-prospect interviews. Claude flags that as a caveat and proceeds. Now it runs Segment 1, Customer Amnesia. It asks: in the customers' own words, what does Synthetic Co do? Then it pulls from the NPS verbatim and surfaces three phrases — 'the spreadsheet replacement,' 'what our paralegals open every morning,' 'the compliance reports we use for audits.' None of those sound like the positioning statement, which is 'the leading legal operations platform for modern firms.' That gap is the audit. Claude scores Description Gap at seven out of ten and moves to Segment 2. This is what the skill does. It does not give you an answer. It produces a sequence of findings, each one defensible, each one referenced to evidence in the intake."

### Shot 4 — DELIVERABLE WALKTHROUGH (60 seconds)

**Camera:** screen-focused. Right half shows `examples/relevancy-audit/deliverable-1.md` (Positioning Decay Map).
**Visual cues:** open the file. Scroll to the buyer language gap section. Highlight the specific finding. Hold the cursor on it for two beats.

**Narration (verbatim):**

"This is deliverable one. The Positioning Decay Map. It is the artifact you walk out of the audit with. I want you to look at one line on this page. It is the buyer language gap row. It says: zero of the top twelve buyer terms appear in our positioning. Zero. Not two. Not five. Zero. Synthetic Co has been to market for seven years and not one of the words their buyers actually use to describe the product is in the sentence the company uses to describe itself. That is the moment in the room where everyone stops talking. The framework was not wrong when they wrote it in 2019. The market moved. The framework stayed. This is real engagement data, masked per our anonymisation rules. Real numbers. Real decay. The Positioning Decay Map is what makes the gap impossible to argue with on a Tuesday morning when the executive team is staring at the slide."
