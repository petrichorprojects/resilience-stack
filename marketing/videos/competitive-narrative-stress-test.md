Gartner reframed the category to 'Agentic Retail Intelligence' in March. Three board members independently asked the same question in the same week. I am pasting their full intake into Claude Code right now. The skill is loaded. Watch what happens. Claude does not jump to a reconstruction. Tessera has all of it. Claude proceeds. Now it runs the Attack Surface Mapping. Layer one — Origin Claim — scored four out of five, holding. Layer two — Differentiation Claim — scored two of five, exposed. Layer three — Proof Claim — scored three of five. Layer four — Outcome Claim — scored two of five, the twelve-million-dollar savings claim has no published methodology. Layer five — Future Claim — scored two of five, 'retail operating system' is now reading as defensive against the new entrant's 'data layer agents need.' Composite Durability thirteen out of twenty-five. Fragile, sliding toward Cracking. Then the Blank Check Test. If the new entrant had unlimited budget, what would they say about Tessera? Claude writes the attack ad. Three lines. Each one lands. This is what the skill does. It produces a sequence of findings, each one defensible, each one referenced to the verbatim evidence in the intake."

### Shot 4 — DELIVERABLE WALKTHROUGH (60 seconds)

**Camera:** screen-focused. Right half shows `examples/competitive-narrative-stress-test/deliverable-3.md` (Reconstructed Narrative).
**Visual cues:** open the file. Scroll to the Future Claim reconstruction. Highlight the before-and-after line. Hold the cursor on it for two beats.

**Narration (verbatim):**

"This is deliverable three. The Reconstructed Narrative. It is the artifact that shows what the rebuilt story sounds like, layer by layer, with the proof anchors that make each claim defensible under sixty-second pressure. I want you to look at one line on this page. Layer five — Future Claim. Before — 'Tessera is the retail operating system for omnichannel commerce.' Read that line cold. It is generic. It is defensive. It does not name what the buyer cares about right now. After — 'Tessera is the retail data layer that agents need to act — without it, every agentic retail tool is hallucinating against stale inventory.' Read it cold. The reframe does three things. It accepts the agentic frame the buyer is already in. It positions Tessera as upstream of the new entrant, not in competition with it. And it surfaces a real failure mode of the new entrant — agents on stale data — that Tessera can prove with telemetry from existing customers. That is what a reconstructed claim looks like. It is not rephrasing. It rests on new evidence. This is real engagement data, masked per our anonymization rules. Real numbers. Real reframe. The Reconstructed Narrative is the artifact that turns the next analyst briefing from defense into offense."
