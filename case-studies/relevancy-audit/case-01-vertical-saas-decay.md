# Relevancy Audit — Vertical SaaS Decay (Case 01)

**Skill**: `relevancy-audit`
**Engagement date**: Q1 2026 (anonymized)
**Duration**: 2.5 hours

## Context

Mid-market vertical SaaS, $30M ARR, vertical: legal operations. Founded 2018, hit PMF 2021, plateaued 2024. New AI-native competitor surfaced 2024, gained share quietly.

## Symptom

- Win rate: 38% → 24% over 2 quarters
- Sales cycle: 47 days → 62 days
- Share of voice: flat 18 months despite 2× marketing spend
- Analyst report: moved from "Leader" to "Established Player"
- 3 lost deals in 1 quarter mentioned a competitor sales did not know existed

## Skill Run

In the room: Founder/CEO, VP Marketing, Head of Sales, Head of Product. Pre-work: 4 quarters of win/loss data + 18 customer interviews + 3 competitor positioning statements.

The workshop classified the company at **Stage 2: Disconnect (borderline Stage 3)**.

## Top 3 Insights

1. **Buyer language drifted.** Of the top 12 terms buyers used in surveys, ZERO appeared in the company's positioning statement.
2. **Sales had no structured competitive intel on the new entrant.** Win/loss reasons were rep-coded; the new competitor's name didn't appear in any loss reason field, but appeared 3× in interview transcripts.
3. **The "AI-native" claim might be marketing not capability.** Required a separate skill (`customer-truth-extraction`) to validate.

## 30 / 60 / 90 Day Outcome

| Window | Action | Measurable result |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| Day 30 | Positioning rewrite using buyer language (6/12 terms now used) | Net-new pipeline +18% MoM |
| Day 60 | Sales playbook now references New Entrant explicitly; win/loss recoded | Loss-rate to New Entrant cut from 60% to 35% |
| Day 90 | Ran `competitive-narrative-stress-test` as follow-up | Sales-led narrative re-recorded; analyst briefing scheduled |

## What We Would Do Differently

- Pre-work survey should have included lapsed prospects, not just current customers — lapsed buyers had a much sharper view of the new entrant.
- Should have insisted on Head of CS in the room — would have surfaced churn risk insights earlier.
