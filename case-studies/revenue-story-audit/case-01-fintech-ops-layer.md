# Revenue Story Audit — Fintech Ops Layer Pre-Series-C (Case 01)

**Skill**: `revenue-story-audit`
**Engagement date**: Q1 2026 (anonymized)
**Duration**: 2.5 hours

## Context

Mid-market B2B SaaS, $20M ARR, fintech operations layer for mid-market neobanks. Founded 2019, hit PMF 2022, Series B-funded. 47 FTEs. Series C kickoff scheduled 90 days out. CFO refused to walk into diligence with a story she could not defend line by line; CEO had been pitching a +120% Y/Y, 115% NRR, "diversified, multi-channel growth" narrative externally. The audit was commissioned to close the gap before the deck went out.

## Symptom

- Board deck claimed "diversified revenue mix" — top 1 customer was 22% of revenue, top 10 were 64%.
- Board deck claimed outbound drove 60% of new ARR — segmented data showed outbound = 40%, inbound = 38%, partner referrals = 22%.
- Forecast in deck: +120% Y/Y. Trailing 4-quarter actual: +47%.
- Stated NRR: 115% ("the team is bullish"). Last full-quarter actual NRR: 96%.
- 3 of top 10 customers in Q4 2025 had churn signals (usage decline, contract renegotiation, exec turnover) — none surfaced in the deck.
- One partner referral source responsible for 38% of new logos in Q1 2026 went silent. Not flagged.
- Expansion and churn reasons in the CRM were rep-coded narrative, not reconciled against usage and billing data.

## Skill Run

In the room: CEO, CFO, Head of Sales. Pre-work pulled: 4 quarters of revenue segmented by customer / channel / product / geography / cohort; last 2 board decks (Q3 2025, Q4 2025); the Q1 2026 sales pitch (deck + recorded call); top 10 deal expansions and top 5 churn risks with rep + reason + dollar amount.

The Board Deck Lie Detector reclassified the deck line by line: "diversified revenue mix" → wrong (top 1 = 22%); "outbound drives 60% of new ARR" → extrapolated (actual 40%); "+120% Y/Y" → hopeful (no traceable dollars supporting >+50%); "115% NRR" → wrong (96% last full quarter). The $1 Trace Exercise on a $480K expansion deal traced cleanly to the named champion and budget cycle — but a second trace on a $345K "multi-year renewal w/ price uplift" surfaced an undisclosed pricing concession that flipped the deal from expansion to flat. Forecast Integrity Score: 3/10 on the deck-as-written. The audit produced a two-version reconciliation — the Internal-Honest version showed +47% actual trajectory with named concentration exposures; the Externally-Defensible version stripped every claim that failed the $1 trace.

## Top 3 Insights

1. **Concentration risk was buried under the "diversified" claim.** Top 1 customer = 22% of revenue; top 10 = 64%. A single logo loss would consume the entire growth budget for the next two quarters. The CFO had not seen the top-10 percentage computed in this form before the audit.
2. **Channel attribution was a story, not a measurement.** Outbound was credited with 60% of new ARR because reps logged opportunities as "outbound" when they first touched them, regardless of true source. Reconciled against first-touch and partner referral data, outbound was 40%, inbound was 38%, and partner referrals — the channel about to go silent — was 22%. The deck had been telling the wrong growth story for two quarters.
3. **NRR optimism was a rep-coded artifact.** The 115% figure came from forward-pipelined expansion deals that had not closed; the 96% actual NRR included the $620K usage-decline account and the $410K renegotiation. CS leadership had not been asked to validate the number before it hit the board.

## 30 / 60 / 90 Day Outcome

| Window | Action | Measurable result |
|--------|--------|--------------------|
| Day 30 | Two-version revenue narrative rolled out internally; CFO replaced deck pages 4-7 with reconciled mechanics map | Forecast Integrity Score rose from 3/10 to 7/10; gap closed to 12% |
| Day 60 | CRM reason codes rebuilt; first-touch attribution implemented; top-10 concentration dashboard standing weekly | Outbound, inbound, partner reported separately and accurately; partner re-engaged after named outreach |
| Day 90 | Series C kickoff held using Externally-Defensible narrative; diligence Q&A pre-rehearsed against the Internal-Honest version | Diligence proceeded without a single "show me the segmented data" rework cycle; term sheet conversations advanced |

## What We Would Do Differently

- Insist on Head of CS in the room from the start. NRR was the single most contested number and CS leadership owns the data that reconciles it; CEO and Head of Sales had to caveat every CS-side claim.
- Run the $1 Trace Exercise on at least three deals, not one. A single trace flattered a single dollar. Three traces surfaced the pricing-concession pattern that would otherwise have hit in diligence.
- Pull partner referral source-of-truth before the session, not during. The silent partner channel surfaced only when the facilitator asked the Head of Sales to name every partner by referral count — should have been pre-pulled and on the wall when leadership walked in.
