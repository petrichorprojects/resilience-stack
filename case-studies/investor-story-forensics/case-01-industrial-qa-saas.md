# Investor Story Forensics — Industrial QA SaaS Pre-Series-C (Case 01)

**Skill**: `investor-story-forensics`
**Engagement date**: Q1 2026 (anonymized)
**Duration**: 2.5 hours

## Context

Vertical SaaS for industrial QA / manufacturing inspection, $42M ARR, Series B-funded, 10-12 months from Series C kickoff. Founded 2018, PMF 2021. 92 FTEs. The CEO had watched two competitors get cratered in diligence when partners pulled cohort retention and called references that contradicted the deck. The forensic was commissioned now, with enough runway to rebuild the narrative around defensible claims before the first partner meeting.

## Symptom

- NRR claim in deck: 130%. Trailing 12-month: 117%. The 130% was the Q3 2025 single best-quarter headline, never re-anchored.
- Customer-count claim: 14 of 25 top global automotive OEMs. Current active: 11 of 25. The 14 was a Q2 2025 snapshot; 3 had churned or paused since.
- Y/Y growth claim: +95%. Trailing actual: +68%. The +95% was the projection set at the start of 2025.
- Non-automotive vertical expansion: 6% of revenue, mostly pilots, 38% pilot churn.
- Reference customers in deck: 5. Current status — 2 champion changes (Q4 2025 and Q1 2026), 1 in renewal pushing for 18% reduction, 1 paused, 1 clean.
- Comparable-set pull (10 vertical SaaS Series C rounds 2024-2025): 5-12x ARR by NRR+growth pair. Deck cited 10x → $400M post. At 117% NRR + 68% growth the defensible multiple was 6-7x → $250-290M post.

## Skill Run

In the room: CEO, CFO, Head of Sales, Head of CS, Head of Product. Pre-work pulled: current deck, last 4 board decks, 4 quarters of cohort retention sliced by initial vertical, customer list w/ status updated Q1 2026, 5 reference customer status one-pagers, Q3 2025 + Q1 2026 forecast vs actuals, 6 quarters non-auto pilot conversion data, comparable-set valuation pull.

Cross-Examination went claim by claim. Why-We-Win held — 4 of 5 founders ex-Toyota/GE/Caterpillar, reference-confirmable. Why-Now held with framing risk — "AI-native" was Q4 2025 vintage; pre-2025 decks said "data-driven inspection." Why-Us cracked: the Q3 2025 board deck exposed the 130% NRR as a single-quarter headline against trailing 117%; the 14 of 25 OEM number traced to a Q2 2025 list slide vs Q1 2026's 11 of 25. Why-This-Trajectory: +95% was projection, +68% trailing actual; 38% non-auto pilot churn named as structural product-build problem (aerospace cert chain, pharma 21 CFR Part 11, semiconductor sub-micron tolerance), not sales. Bear Case Pre-Mortem produced 4 short theses: NRR best-quarter-cited-as-trailing → 10x-to-6-7x multiple delta; auto OEM concentration at 94% of revenue; structural pilot churn; 3 of 5 references not ready for cold call. Split-Room Coherence: CEO+CFO+Sales told one story; CS+Product told another — divergence point was auto OEM concentration. Narrative Integrity Score: Why-We-Win 4/5, Why-Now 3/5, Why-Us 2/5, Why-This-Trajectory 2/5, Why-This-Price 2/5. Composite 13/25.

## Top 3 Insights

1. **Why-Us was partially false in a way the team had stopped noticing.** The 130% NRR was written into the Q3 2025 board deck as the headline for that quarter — true on a single-quarter basis. It migrated into the investor deck as the trailing number without anyone re-anchoring it. The 14 of 25 OEM count was a Q2 2025 snapshot never updated when 3 customers churned or paused. Neither was a lie — both were artifacts of a deck not re-anchored in 6 months. A partner pulling cohort retention surfaces both inside 30 minutes.
2. **Why-This-Trajectory was vulnerable because non-auto pilot churn is a product problem, not a sales problem.** 38% pilot churn traced to structural product gaps requiring 12-24 month engineering investment, not 90-day sales playbooks. The team-drafted bear case framed it as "normal early-vertical churn" — sophisticated investors recognize the distinction and discount accordingly.
3. **The reference customer arc was not ready and the team did not know.** 3 of 5 named references were not ready to take a cold diligence call. The deck listed all 5 as proof points. Reference regrooming is a 60-90 day exercise that cannot be done inside diligence; it had to be done now, with the Series C 10-12 months out.

## 30 / 60 / 90 Day Outcome

| Window | Action | Measurable result |
|--------|--------|--------------------|
| Day 30 | Deck re-anchored to trailing data: NRR 117% trailing with 124% auto OEM cohort and 78% non-auto pilot cohort broken out; customer count 11 of 25 OEMs active (3 churned/paused named, no hiding); +68% trailing growth with +95% labeled as projection on a separate slide; non-auto reframed as 12-24 month product roadmap | Board pre-briefed; no surprises at next quarterly. Split-Room test re-run; divergence dropped to 1 of 5 claim classes |
| Day 60 | Reference customer regrooming: new VPs at Auto OEM A and Auto OEM E pre-briefed via QBR cycle; relationship deepened with Tier-1 supplier D; 2 replacement references arc-built inside active base | 4 of 5 references ready for cold diligence call (was 1 of 5) |
| Day 90 | Why-This-Price re-anchored to comparable-set pull at defensible numbers: target Series C at $280M post, 6.7x trailing ARR, with $400M reserved as upside case dependent on hitting projection. Pre-pitched to 2 friendly Series C investors as calibration | Narrative Integrity Score rebuilt to 21/25. First partner meeting scheduled month 10 with no narrative-reality gap to defend |

## What We Would Do Differently

- Pull current reference customer relationship status FIRST. The reference arc was the longest remediation (60-90 days) and highest-risk gap; it was pulled mid-session because the team assumed "they're still customers" was sufficient.
- Include Head of CS from Phase 1. CS sees renewal pressure and paused accounts first; including CS from the start would have surfaced the Why-Us gap in the first 30 minutes.
- Board deck → investor deck migration is the most common path for a best-quarter-cited-as-trailing gap. Recommend a standing "deck migration review" 90 days before any raise — verify every metric against the trailing source of truth, not the most recent board deck.
