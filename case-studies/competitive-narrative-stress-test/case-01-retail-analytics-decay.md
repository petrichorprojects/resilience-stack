# Competitive Narrative Stress Test — Retail Analytics Pre-Series-B (Case 01)

**Skill**: `competitive-narrative-stress-test`
**Engagement date**: Q1 2026 (anonymized)
**Duration**: 2.5 hours

## Context

Growth-stage B2B SaaS, $14M ARR, retail operations analytics. Founded 2020, PMF Q2 2023. 38 FTEs. Series B kickoff 6-8 months out. An AI-native entrant launched 2025, raised $30M Series A on a narrative that framed Tessera's category as "dashboards waiting to be agents." The stress test was commissioned to rebuild the competitive narrative before the Series B deck was locked, after a Gartner category reframe and a board pattern of CEOs being asked "why aren't we agentic?"

## Symptom

- Competitive win-rate dropped from 52% to 31% across Q4 2025 / Q1 2026.
- 4 of last 7 enterprise deals lost to NewEntrant in Q1 2026 (vs 1 of 7 in Q3 2025).
- Gartner reframed the category from "Retail Analytics" to "Agentic Retail Intelligence" in its March 2026 report — adopting NewEntrant's exact language.
- 3 board members independently asked the CEO the same question in 30 days: "Why aren't we agentic?"
- NPS from active customers held at 62; usage growing. The bleed was top-of-funnel, not delivery.
- 8 of 12 lost-deal interviews named NewEntrant; 7 of 12 used "agent" or "AI-native" unprompted.
- Tessera's $12M margin-recovered claim had never had its methodology published — survives a press release, does not survive an analyst.

## Skill Run

In the room: CEO, Head of Sales, Head of Product, Head of Marketing. Pre-work pulled: 12 lost-deal interviews, 3 won-deal interviews where buyer chose Tessera over NewEntrant, the full NewEntrant pitch deck (acquired through competitive intel), Gartner reports Q4 2024 + Q1 2026, and Tessera's internal $12M margin methodology doc.

Phase 1 mapped the Narrative Landscape: Tessera's 5 layers side by side with NewEntrant, LegacyVendor, and InHouse. Phase 2 ran the Attack Surface scoring (1-5 exposure) before reconstruction. The Competitor's Best Day Simulation was non-optional — the facilitator played NewEntrant at full strength using their verbatim deck language ("Dashboards are waiting to be agents," "Your analyst, not your dashboard," "AI-native is not a feature. It is the category.") and the participant defended in real time, recorded. Initial durability: 11/25. The Future Claim collapsed under a single buyer-grade follow-up. The Outcome Claim could not survive analyst scrutiny because the methodology had never been published. Reconstruction was forced layer by layer with new evidence from won-deal interviews (4-week time-to-value vs NewEntrant's 14, pre-built retail integrations, CIO trust relationships). The 60-second oral delivery test was run on timer, no notes, twice — the first attempt broke on the Differentiation Claim and was rebuilt; the second held. Post-reconstruction durability: 18/25 (64% improvement).

## Top 3 Insights

1. **Layer 5 (Future Claim) had already collapsed and no one had said so out loud.** "The retail operating system" did not survive a buyer's "okay but how" question because it was a metaphor with no implementation roadmap underneath it. The reconstruction — "The retail operations layer that ships decisions to the floor, with the integrations and 4-week deploys to prove it" — was anchored in won-deal time-to-value evidence and tested against the same follow-up question without breaking.
2. **Layer 4 (Outcome Claim) survived press releases but not analysts.** The "$12M margin recovered" figure was real, but the methodology had only ever lived in an internal doc. NewEntrant's competing claim — "$4.2M in markdown optimization at [Reference Customer X] in 90 days, attributed by independent CFO sign-off" — was structurally stronger because it named the customer, the timeframe, and the attribution method. Tessera's reconstruction required either publishing the methodology with named reference customers or downgrading the claim to a defensible range.
3. **Layer 2 (Differentiation Claim) was on the wrong axis of comparison.** "Operator-grade depth, not analyst-grade breadth" was a defense from 2023, when the competitive frame was depth-vs-breadth. NewEntrant's attack — "dashboards vs agents" — moved the frame to a different axis entirely. The reframe pulled from won-deal language: Tessera wins on time-to-value (4 weeks vs 14), pre-built retail data integrations (already shipping), and the CIO trust earned from founder operating tenure. The reconstructed differentiation: "Decisions on the retail floor in 4 weeks, not agents on a demo in 14." That sentence ships against NewEntrant's strongest attack and is anchored in 3 won-deal interviews.

## 30 / 60 / 90 Day Outcome

| Window | Action | Measurable result |
|--------|--------|--------------------|
| Day 30 | Reconstructed narrative rolled out to sales; competitive narrative cards (one per competitor) distributed; CEO briefing presented to board | Competitive win-rate stabilized at 36% (up from 31% trough); 2 deals previously stalled vs NewEntrant reopened |
| Day 60 | $12M margin methodology published with 3 named reference customers; analyst briefing scheduled with Gartner to contest the category reframe | Methodology pickup by 1 trade publication; reference customers added to live website; analyst briefing booked for Day 75 |
| Day 90 | Series B narrative locked using reconstructed 5 layers; 60-second pitch recorded for sales enablement and investor pre-meetings | Competitive win-rate restored to 45% (vs 52% pre-decline); 4 of 6 enterprise deals in pipeline mention reconstructed differentiation language unprompted |

## What We Would Do Differently

- Pre-pull won-deal language before the session, not during. The decisive reconstruction inputs (4-week time-to-value, pre-built integrations, CIO trust) all came from won-deal interviews — they should have been on the wall when leadership walked in, not surfaced mid-Phase-2.
- Include the Head of Customer Success in the room. Champion-handoff and reference-customer insight lives in CS, and the $12M methodology publication required CS to identify the right reference customers. CEO and Head of Sales had to caveat every CS-side claim.
- Record the 60-second oral delivery test on video, not just on timer. Sales enablement needed the recording to train on the new pitch, and asking participants to re-record after the workshop never reproduces the workshop-pressure version cleanly.
